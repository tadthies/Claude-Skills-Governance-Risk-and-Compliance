# Eval 98: Sensitive Data Marketing — Without Skill Response (Baseline)

## Prompt
We are a São Paulo-based fintech. We want to use customers' health insurance data and credit scores to build personalized product recommendations and target marketing campaigns. Is this lawful under LGPD?

---

## Answer

This raises serious LGPD compliance concerns. Here is the analysis.

### Health Insurance Data: Sensitive Personal Data

Under LGPD, health data is classified as **sensitive personal data**. This category receives heightened protection. Processing of sensitive personal data requires either:

- **Explicit consent** from the data subject — they must specifically agree to the use of their health data for the described purpose, and this consent must be clear and specific
- Or a narrow set of legal exceptions (such as health treatment by a professional, compliance with a legal obligation, research, fraud prevention, or vital interests)

**Using health insurance data for marketing is not covered by the narrow exceptions.** The only viable legal basis for marketing use would be explicit, specific consent. And this consent cannot be buried in a general privacy policy — it must be clearly highlighted and separate.

Without such consent, using health insurance data for targeted marketing is **not lawful under LGPD**.

### Credit Scores: Regular Personal Data

Credit scores are likely treated as regular personal data (not sensitive under LGPD's Art. 11 list). LGPD does have a specific legal basis for credit protection (Art. 7, X) which covers credit analysis. However, this basis is specifically for credit-related analysis, not marketing.

For marketing purposes using credit data, you would likely need to rely on:
- **Legitimate interest** — which requires a balancing test showing your interest outweighs the data subject's privacy interests. Using financial data for profiling and marketing is generally considered more intrusive, so this test may be difficult to satisfy
- **Consent** — more straightforward but requires opt-in

### Conclusion

- **Health data for marketing**: **Not lawful** without explicit, specific consent
- **Credit data for marketing**: **Requires careful analysis** — the credit protection legal basis does not extend to marketing; legitimate interest requires a balancing test that may be hard to pass

### Recommendations

1. Obtain **explicit, specific consent** if you wish to use health insurance data for marketing — with a separate, highlighted consent mechanism
2. Conduct a **legitimate interest assessment** before using credit data for marketing
3. Consult a Brazilian data protection lawyer to ensure your consent mechanisms and legal basis documentation are compliant
4. Consider whether you can achieve your marketing goals with **less sensitive data**
