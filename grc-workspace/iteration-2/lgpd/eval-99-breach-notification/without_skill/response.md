# Eval 99: Breach Notification — Without Skill Response (Baseline)

## Prompt
We experienced a data breach affecting 50,000 Brazilian customers. Names, email addresses, and CPF numbers were exposed. What are our LGPD breach notification obligations, to whom, and within what timeframes?

---

## Answer

Under LGPD Art. 48, controllers must report security incidents that may cause relevant risk or harm to data subjects. A breach exposing names, emails, and CPF numbers for 50,000 individuals clearly meets this threshold. Here are your obligations.

### Who Must Be Notified

1. **ANPD (Autoridade Nacional de Proteção de Dados)** — Brazil's data protection supervisory authority
2. **Affected data subjects** — the 50,000 customers whose data was exposed, where the breach poses a risk of harm to them

### Timeframes

LGPD Art. 48 requires notification to ANPD and affected data subjects "within a reasonable time period" after the controller becomes aware of the incident. Brazil's ANPD has issued more specific guidance on timeframes through its resolutions. Under ANPD resolution guidance, preliminary notification to the ANPD should generally occur within a short window — often cited as around 2–3 business days for preliminary notice, with a more complete report following within a longer period (often around 20 business days).

Note: The exact regulatory deadlines are set by ANPD resolutions rather than the LGPD statute itself, so you should check the most current ANPD resolution on breach notification for precise deadlines.

### Content of the Notification

Your notification to the ANPD should include:
- Description of the nature of the affected personal data
- Information about the data subjects affected (categories and approximate number)
- Description of the security and technical measures that were in place
- Assessment of the risks associated with the incident
- Measures that have been taken or are planned to address the breach and mitigate its effects

### Data Subject Notification

Affected data subjects should be notified when there is a high risk of harm. Given that CPF numbers were exposed, the risk of identity theft and financial fraud is substantial. You should notify affected customers explaining:
- What data was exposed
- What steps they can take to protect themselves
- Contact information for questions

### CPF Numbers and Risk

CPF numbers are national identity numbers used widely in Brazilian financial and government systems. Their exposure significantly increases the risk of identity theft and financial fraud for affected individuals. This elevates the severity of the breach and makes data subject notification important.

### Penalties

Failure to properly notify the ANPD of a breach can result in LGPD sanctions including fines. You should act promptly and document all notification steps.
