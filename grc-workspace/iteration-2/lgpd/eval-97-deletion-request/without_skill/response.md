# Eval 97: Deletion Request — Without Skill Response (Baseline)

## Prompt
A Brazilian customer has requested deletion of all their personal data from our CRM system, email marketing platform, and analytics tool. How do we handle this under LGPD? Are there any exceptions?

---

## Answer

Under LGPD, data subjects have the right to request deletion of their personal data. Here is how to handle it.

### Steps to Process the Request

1. **Verify the requester's identity** before taking any action to prevent unauthorized deletion requests.

2. **Acknowledge the request** and begin the process of locating all personal data across your systems — CRM, email marketing platform, and analytics tool.

3. **Delete the data** from all relevant systems within a reasonable timeframe. LGPD does not set an explicit number of days in the statutory text for deletion specifically, but you should act without undue delay — generally within 15 days is considered the standard.

4. **Instruct your service providers** (the email marketing platform, analytics tool) to also delete the individual's data. As a controller, you are responsible for ensuring your processors act on your instructions.

5. **Confirm to the data subject** that deletion has been completed, or explain any exceptions that apply.

### Exceptions Under LGPD

LGPD does provide exceptions where you may retain data even after a deletion request. These include:

- **Legal obligation**: If you are required by law to retain certain data (e.g., tax records, financial transaction data for regulatory purposes), you may do so.
- **Judicial or administrative proceedings**: If the data is needed for an ongoing legal case or proceedings.
- **Fraud prevention**: Data may be retained where necessary to detect or prevent fraud or protect the data subject in credit operations.
- **Research purposes**: Anonymized data used for research may be retained.
- **Protection of life**: If retaining the data is necessary to protect someone's life or physical safety.

### Consent vs. Other Legal Bases

If the data was collected based on consent, the data subject's request to delete is closely tied to consent revocation. When consent is withdrawn, processing based on that consent must stop and the data should typically be deleted unless another exception applies. For data processed under other legal bases (contract, legal obligation), the exceptions above are more likely to apply and allow some retention.

### Documentation

Keep a record of the deletion request, the steps you took, and the outcome. This is important for demonstrating accountability to the ANPD if any questions arise.
