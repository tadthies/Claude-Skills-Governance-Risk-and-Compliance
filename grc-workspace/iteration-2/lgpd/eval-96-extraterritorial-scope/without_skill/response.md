# Eval 96: Extraterritorial Scope — Without Skill Response (Baseline)

## Prompt
We are a US-based SaaS company. We have customers in Brazil — we collect their name, email address, CPF (Brazilian tax ID), and purchase history. We have no physical presence in Brazil; all processing happens on US servers. Does LGPD apply to us?

---

## Answer

**Yes, LGPD likely applies to your company**, even without physical presence in Brazil. Here is why.

### LGPD's Scope

Brazil's Lei Geral de Proteção de Dados (LGPD), Law No. 13,709/2018, has extraterritorial reach. It applies when:
- The data processing is carried out in Brazil
- The purpose is to offer or supply goods or services to individuals located in Brazil
- The personal data was collected in Brazil

Your company falls under at least one of these criteria — you are actively offering a SaaS service to Brazilian customers and collecting their personal data. The fact that your servers are in the US does not exempt you.

### CPF as Personal Data

The CPF (Cadastro de Pessoas Físicas) is a national identification number for Brazilian citizens. It constitutes personal data under LGPD and its collection/processing triggers compliance obligations. Depending on context, it may create heightened sensitivity given its role in financial identity.

### Key Obligations That Would Apply

If LGPD applies (which it does), you would need to:

- **Identify a legal basis** for each processing activity (consent, contract performance, legitimate interest, etc.)
- **Provide a privacy notice** to data subjects explaining your processing activities
- **Honor data subject rights** — access, correction, deletion, portability, and more
- **Appoint a Data Protection Officer** (called "Encarregado" under LGPD)
- **Implement security measures** to protect personal data
- **Notify** the ANPD (Brazil's data protection authority) of significant data breaches

### Penalties

The ANPD can impose fines for non-compliance. Penalties under LGPD can reach up to 2% of the company's revenue in Brazil, with a maximum cap per violation.

### Recommendation

You should conduct an LGPD compliance assessment, establish appropriate legal bases for all data processing activities involving Brazilian customers, update your privacy notice to meet LGPD requirements, and consider the implications of your international data transfers (data flowing from Brazil to your US servers requires a valid transfer mechanism).
