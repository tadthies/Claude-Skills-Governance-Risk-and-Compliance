# Handling a Consent Withdrawal Request Under Vietnam's PDPL

When a Vietnamese customer wants to withdraw consent, here's what the Law (No. 91/2025/QH15) and Decree 356/2025/ND-CP require.

## 1. The Right Itself

Withdrawal of consent is an explicit data subject right under **Article 4(b)** of the Law: data subjects may give, withhold, or withdraw consent **at any time**. This is a standing right — you cannot condition it, limit it to certain windows, or require a justification from the customer.

## 2. Process

1. **Verify the requestor's identity** before acting on the request — this protects against fraudulent withdrawal requests made by someone other than the actual data subject.
2. **Acknowledge the request within 2 working days** (Decree 356 Article 5 — this acknowledgement timeframe applies to any data subject request, including withdrawal).
3. **Determine what processing must stop.** If the customer gave layered/purpose-specific consent (as required under Article 9), confirm exactly which consent is being withdrawn — e.g., withdrawing marketing consent doesn't necessarily withdraw consent for account/transactional processing unless the customer says so.
4. **Cease the relevant processing** within the fulfilment deadline (see below).
5. **Document the request and your response** — Decree 356 requires all data subject requests and responses to be logged for audit purposes.

## 3. Timeframe

| Step | Deadline |
|---|---|
| Acknowledge the withdrawal request | **2 working days** |
| Fulfil the withdrawal (cease processing) | **15 working days** (Decree 356 Article 5 — same timeframe applies to withdrawal of consent and restriction of processing) |
| Extension | One extension is permitted; you must notify the customer of the extension and reason within the original deadline |

Note: this 15-working-day fulfilment window is specifically the timeframe for withdrawal/restriction requests — it's distinct from the 10-working-day window for access/correction requests and the 20-working-day window for deletion requests. If the customer's withdrawal is bundled with a request to also delete their data, you're working two clocks: 15 working days to stop processing under the withdrawn consent, and 20 working days to complete any associated deletion.

## 4. Consequences of Withdrawal

- **Prospective only — not retroactive.** Withdrawal does **not** affect the lawfulness of processing that occurred before the withdrawal. Any processing you did under valid consent prior to the withdrawal date remains lawful; you don't need to "undo" it or treat it as having been unlawful.
- **You must stop the specific processing covered by the withdrawn consent going forward**, within the 15-working-day fulfilment window.
- **Other lawful bases are unaffected.** If some of the processing relies on a basis other than consent (e.g., contract performance under Article 19 — necessary to fulfil an order the customer already placed, or a statutory retention obligation such as financial transaction record-keeping), you may continue that specific processing on that separate basis even after consent withdrawal. Be precise about which processing activities were consent-based versus contract-based, since withdrawal only kills the consent-based ones.
- **Withdrawal may affect service availability.** If the withdrawn consent underpins a core service feature (e.g., a personalisation feature the customer explicitly opted into), you may need to disable that feature for the customer — but you cannot use this as leverage to discourage withdrawal, and you cannot make continued access to the core service conditional on maintaining unrelated consents.
- **No penalty or discrimination against the customer** for withdrawing — consistent with the "voluntary" consent principle (Article 9), withdrawal must be as easy and consequence-free as giving consent, aside from the practical loss of features tied to that specific consent.

## 5. Practical Recommendations

- Provide a **self-service withdrawal mechanism** (account settings/preference centre) that mirrors how consent was originally given — this satisfies the "as easy to withdraw as to consent" expectation implicit in the voluntariness principle, and reduces your operational burden versus handling every withdrawal manually.
- Maintain a **consent/withdrawal audit trail**: original consent record, withdrawal request timestamp, identity verification method, processing activities stopped, and completion date.
- If withdrawal triggers a data deletion request as well, run the deletion process in parallel and track it against its own 20-working-day deadline — don't let it get absorbed into the 15-day withdrawal clock.
- Train customer support staff to recognize withdrawal requests made through any channel (email, in-app, phone) — the request doesn't need to use formal language; any clear indication the customer wants to stop the processing counts.

## Summary Timeline

**Day 0:** Customer requests withdrawal → verify identity → **Day 2 (working):** acknowledge receipt → **Day 15 (working):** processing under that consent must have ceased, with documentation completed throughout.

*This is general compliance guidance, not legal advice. If the withdrawal request is contested, overlaps with a legal retention obligation, or involves a dispute about scope, consult qualified Vietnamese counsel before responding.*
