## Conclusion: Yes, CSRD Applies

Your company falls under the Corporate Sustainability Reporting Directive (CSRD, EU Directive 2022/2464/EU) and is subject to mandatory sustainability reporting obligations.

## Legal Basis and Threshold Analysis

The CSRD establishes a tiered applicability framework based on company size and listing status. Three size criteria are used, of which a company must exceed **at least two** to qualify as a "large" company:

| Criterion | CSRD Threshold | Your Company | Exceeded? |
|-----------|---------------|--------------|-----------|
| Employees | > 250 | 800 | Yes |
| Net turnover | > €40M | €120M | Yes |
| Total assets | > €20M | €55M | Yes |

Your company exceeds **all three** thresholds, qualifying as a **large undertaking** under Article 3 of the EU Accounting Directive (2013/34/EU) as amended by CSRD.

Additionally, your company is **listed on a regulated market** (Frankfurt Stock Exchange), which is relevant to the phased rollout timeline.

## Reporting Timeline

The CSRD introduced a phased implementation schedule:

### Phase 1 — Already in scope (financial year 2024, reports due 2025)
Large public-interest entities (PIEs) with more than 500 employees that were already subject to the Non-Financial Reporting Directive (NFRD). This phase does **not** apply to your company, as you have 800 employees but were likely not previously a PIE subject to NFRD (NFRD applied to PIEs with >500 employees, but your company's prior NFRD status would need to be confirmed).

### Phase 2 — **Your company's applicable phase** (financial year 2025, reports due 2026)
Large undertakings not previously subject to NFRD — meaning large companies (meeting at least two of the three size thresholds above) that were **not** already reporting under NFRD. This covers listed large companies like yours with 250–500+ employees not previously in scope.

**Your first CSRD report will cover financial year 2025, to be published in 2026.**

> Note: If your company was already subject to NFRD as a PIE with >500 employees prior to CSRD, you would fall under Phase 1 (reporting on FY 2024). Given that NFRD historically applied to PIEs with >500 employees, your company (with 800 employees and Frankfurt listing) may warrant a careful review of prior NFRD status. If previously subject to NFRD, your first reporting year would be **2024** (report due **2025**). If not previously subject to NFRD, your first reporting year is **2025** (report due **2026**).

## Key Reporting Requirements Under CSRD

Once in scope, your company must:

1. **Report in accordance with European Sustainability Reporting Standards (ESRS)** — mandatory sector-agnostic standards (ESRS 1 & 2) and topical standards covering environment, social, and governance matters.

2. **Apply double materiality assessment** — disclosures must cover both financial materiality (sustainability risks affecting the company) and impact materiality (the company's impacts on people and environment).

3. **Include sustainability statement in the management report** — sustainability disclosures must be integrated into (not appended to) the annual report.

4. **Obtain limited assurance** — initially required at a "limited assurance" level, transitioning to "reasonable assurance" in future years.

5. **Ensure machine-readable reporting** — the annual report, including the sustainability statement, must be tagged using the ESEF (European Single Electronic Format) taxonomy.

6. **Value chain disclosure** — you will need to gather data from your upstream and downstream value chain to the extent material.

## German Transposition

Germany transposed the CSRD into national law through the **Umsetzungsgesetz zur CSRD** (CSRD Implementation Act), which amended the Handelsgesetzbuch (HGB). German companies must file the Nachhaltigkeitsbericht (sustainability report) as part of the Lagebericht (management report) and submit it to the Bundesanzeiger (Federal Gazette).

## Recommended Next Steps

1. **Confirm NFRD status** to determine whether Phase 1 (FY2024) or Phase 2 (FY2025) applies.
2. **Conduct a double materiality assessment** to identify which ESRS topics are material for your manufacturing operations.
3. **Establish data collection processes** for environmental (e.g., emissions, energy, water), social (e.g., workforce, supply chain labor), and governance metrics.
4. **Engage auditors early** to prepare for the limited assurance requirement.
5. **Review ESRS sector-specific standards** relevant to manufacturing once finalized by EFRAG.
6. **Assess value chain data availability**, particularly for Scope 3 emissions and supplier social standards.
