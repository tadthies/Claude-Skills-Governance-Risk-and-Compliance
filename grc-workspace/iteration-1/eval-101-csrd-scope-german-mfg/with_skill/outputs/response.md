## Scope Determination

**Legal basis:** Art. 19a and Art. 29a of Directive (EU) 2022/2464 (CSRD), amending Accounting Directive 2013/34/EU.

### Step 1 — Entity Type

You are an EU-incorporated company (Germany) listed on an EU regulated market (Frankfurt Stock Exchange / Frankfurter Wertpapierbörse). This places you squarely within the CSRD's EU entity rules and triggers consideration under both the "large company" and "listed SME" categories.

### Step 2 — Large Company Size Thresholds

CSRD defines a **large company** as one meeting **at least 2 of the following 3 criteria** (Accounting Directive Art. 3(4), as referenced in CSRD):

| Criterion | Threshold | Your Company | Meets? |
|-----------|-----------|-------------|--------|
| Employees | > 250 | 800 | YES |
| Annual net turnover | > €40M | €120M | YES |
| Total assets (balance sheet) | > €20M | €55M | YES |

**Result: You meet all three criteria — you are unambiguously a large company.**

### Step 3 — PIE Status Check

A Public Interest Entity (PIE) includes companies listed on an EU regulated market. The Frankfurt Stock Exchange is an EU regulated market. You are therefore a **listed PIE**.

However, the first wave of mandatory reporters under CSRD was limited to large PIEs **already subject to NFRD** — i.e., those with **more than 500 employees**. With 800 employees, you exceed the 500-employee threshold.

### Step 4 — NFRD Status Check

The Non-Financial Reporting Directive (NFRD, Directive 2014/95/EU) applied to large PIEs with more than 500 employees. With 800 employees and listing on a regulated market, your company **was subject to NFRD** and therefore falls into the **Wave 1 (large PIEs > 500 employees)** category under CSRD.

---

## Verdict

**YES — you are in scope for CSRD.**

You qualify under the most accelerated reporting timeline as a **large PIE with more than 500 employees** (previously subject to NFRD).

## Key Immediate Obligations (Art. 19a CSRD / ESRS)

As a Wave 1 reporter, you are required to comply with the **full ESRS framework** from FY 2024:

1. **ESRS 2 — General Disclosures (mandatory):** Governance (GOV-1 to GOV-5), Strategy (SBM-1 to SBM-3), IRO management (IRO-1). No materiality exemption applies to ESRS 2.

2. **Double Materiality Assessment (DMA):** Must be conducted in accordance with ESRS 1 (paras. 45–56) across all environmental (E1–E5), social (S1–S4), and governance (G1) topics, covering own operations and value chain.

3. **Topical ESRS Standards:** Apply to all topics assessed as material through the DMA. For a German manufacturer, ESRS E1 (Climate Change — Scope 1/2/3 emissions, transition plan), ESRS S1 (Own Workforce), and ESRS G1 (Business Conduct) are highly likely to be material.

4. **Third-Party Assurance (Art. 26a):** Limited assurance required from the first reporting year. Your statutory auditor or an accredited independent assurance services provider (IASP) must provide assurance on the sustainability disclosures.

5. **XBRL Digital Tagging:** Sustainability disclosures must be tagged in iXBRL format under ESEF (European Single Electronic Format).

6. **Location:** Disclosures must appear in a **dedicated section of the management report** (Lagebericht under HGB / §289b et seq.), not in a standalone document.

## Important Note: CSRD Omnibus Proposal (2025)

The European Commission published a CSRD Omnibus Package in February 2025 proposing potential scope narrowing and datapoint reductions. As of the current date (May 2026), you should verify the current legislative status of any adopted amendments, as these may affect the specific datapoints required. However, **your in-scope status as a large listed PIE with > 500 employees is unlikely to be affected by any proposed simplifications**, which primarily target smaller in-scope companies.
