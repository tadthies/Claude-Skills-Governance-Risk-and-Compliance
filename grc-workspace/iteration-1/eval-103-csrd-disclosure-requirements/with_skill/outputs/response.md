## 1. ESRS 2 — General Disclosures (Mandatory for All In-Scope Companies)

ESRS 2 is **fully mandatory regardless of your DMA outcome**. Even if every topical standard had been found non-material, ESRS 2 would still be required in full (ESRS 2, para. 1; Commission Delegated Regulation (EU) 2023/2772).

### Governance (GOV)

**GOV-1 — Role of governance bodies**
- Composition and sustainability expertise of your board and supervisory bodies
- How the board oversees sustainability strategy, impacts, risks, and opportunities (IROs)
- Sustainability topics on board agendas and reporting lines into governance

**GOV-2 — Information provided to governance bodies**
- Frequency and nature of sustainability reporting to the board/management
- How governance bodies factor sustainability risks and opportunities into decisions

**GOV-3 — Sustainability in incentive schemes**
- Whether and how ESG performance metrics are integrated into executive/management remuneration
- Proportion of remuneration linked to sustainability targets

**GOV-4 — Due diligence statement**
- Whether your due diligence process aligns with international standards (UN Guiding Principles on Business and Human Rights; OECD Guidelines for Multinational Enterprises)
- How human rights and environmental due diligence is embedded in operations

**GOV-5 — Risk management and internal controls**
- Your internal control framework for sustainability reporting
- How you manage risks of material misstatement in sustainability data

### Strategy and Business Model (SBM)

**SBM-1 — Strategy, business model, and value chain**
- Overview of your business model and key value drivers
- How sustainability matters are integrated into strategy
- Description of your full value chain (upstream suppliers, own operations, downstream)
- Significant products/services, markets, and geographies

**SBM-2 — Stakeholder engagement**
- Which stakeholders were engaged and how
- How stakeholder views influenced your strategy and materiality conclusions
- Linkage between stakeholder input and identified material IROs

**SBM-3 — Material IROs and interaction with strategy**
- Output of your DMA: list of material topics (E1, S1, G1) and why they are material
- How material IROs interact with your business model and strategy
- Current and anticipated financial effects of material IROs
- Resilience of strategy under different scenarios

### Impacts, Risks and Opportunities (IRO)

**IRO-1 — DMA process description**
- Your DMA methodology: scope, process, assessment criteria
- How you assessed impact significance (scale, scope, irremediability, likelihood)
- How you identified and assessed financial risks/opportunities
- Stakeholders consulted and how their input shaped conclusions
- Why non-material topics were excluded (materiality statement)

## 2. ESRS E1 — Climate Change (Material)

Climate change is material for your company. This is one of the most disclosure-intensive ESRS standards. **Note:** Even for companies that find climate change non-material, Art. 19a(2)(a) of the CSRD legally requires disclosure of a transition plan — so this standard carries special weight.

### E1-1 — Transition plan for climate change mitigation
- Decarbonisation targets for 2030, 2040, and 2050 aligned with the 1.5°C pathway
- Planned actions and enablers (technology, operational changes, procurement)
- Financial resources allocated: capex, opex, and R&D for decarbonisation
- Role of carbon offsets (must be distinguished from actual emission reductions)
- Locked-in GHG assets and stranded asset risk assessment
- EU Taxonomy alignment targets

### E1-2 — Policies related to climate change mitigation and adaptation
- Scope of your climate policies (own operations and/or value chain)
- Climate risk management policies
- Net-zero ambition and decarbonisation pathway

### E1-3 — Actions and resources
- Decarbonisation action plan with timelines
- Resources committed (financial, human, technical)
- Actions addressing key GHG sources in own operations and value chain

### E1-4 — Targets related to climate change
- GHG reduction targets (Scope 1, 2, and 3 separately)
- Alignment with Paris Agreement (1.5°C pathway)
- Energy efficiency and renewable energy targets
- Physical and transition risk mitigation targets

### E1-5 — Energy consumption and mix
- Total energy consumption (MWh)
- Breakdown: fossil fuels / renewables / nuclear
- Energy intensity (per net revenue or other relevant denominator)
- Renewable energy share (%)

### E1-6 — Gross Scope 1, 2, 3 and total GHG emissions
This is typically the most data-intensive disclosure:
- **Scope 1:** Direct GHG emissions (tCO2e) from owned/controlled sources
- **Scope 2:** Indirect GHG emissions — both **location-based** and **market-based** (tCO2e)
- **Scope 3:** All **15 GHG Protocol categories** (tCO2e) — upstream and downstream
- Total GHG emissions (Scope 1 + 2 + 3)
- GHG intensity (tCO2e per € net revenue)
- Methodology disclosure: GHG Protocol alignment, emission factors used, exclusions

*Phase-in relief: Scope 3 data may be phased in during the first reporting year (ESRS 1, paras. 118–134). Confirm current phase-in status.*

### E1-7 — GHG removals and climate project finance
- GHG removals from own operations (forestry, CCUS, etc.)
- Carbon credits purchased (volume, type, standard applied)
- Clear distinction between removals and purchased offsets in any net-zero claims

### E1-8 — Internal carbon pricing
- Whether an internal carbon price is used in investment/operational decisions
- Price per tCO2e and scope of application

### E1-9 — Anticipated financial effects from climate risks and opportunities
- Financial effects of **physical risks** (acute: flooding, storms; chronic: temperature rise, sea-level rise)
- Financial effects of **transition risks** (policy/regulatory, technology, market, reputational)
- Climate-related opportunities and anticipated financial benefits
- Climate scenario analysis (EFRAG recommends at minimum 1.5°C and >2°C scenarios)

## 3. ESRS S1 — Own Workforce (Material)

### S1-1 — Policies related to own workforce
- Working conditions policies (health & safety, working hours, wages)
- Equal treatment and non-discrimination policies
- Human rights policies related to workforce

### S1-2 — Processes for engaging with own workers
- How workers and their representatives (trade unions, works councils) are consulted
- Topics covered in engagement and outcomes

### S1-3 — Processes to remediate negative impacts
- Grievance mechanisms available to workers
- How workers can raise concerns (whistleblowing channels, anonymous reporting)

### S1-4 — Actions and effectiveness
- Actions taken on material workforce impacts
- Approaches to managing workforce risks and opportunities
- Assessment of effectiveness of actions taken

### S1-5 — Targets
- Specific measurable targets to address material negative impacts and manage risks

### S1-6 — Employee characteristics (Quantitative)
- Total headcount
- Breakdown by gender
- By country (for material operations)
- By contract type: permanent vs. temporary
- By employment regime: full-time vs. part-time
- Number of non-employee workers (contractors, agency staff)

### S1-7 — Characteristics of non-employees
- Number and profile of non-employee workforce

### S1-8 — Collective bargaining coverage
- % of own employees covered by collective bargaining agreements
- Countries/regions where coverage applies

### S1-9 — Diversity metrics
- % female employees across total workforce and management levels
- % employees by age group (<30, 30–50, >50)

### S1-10 — Adequate wages
- Whether employee remuneration meets or exceeds living wage benchmarks

### S1-11 — Social protection coverage
- Access to social protection (sick pay, parental leave, pension) for employees

### S1-12 — Persons with disabilities
- % or number of employees with disabilities

### S1-13 — Training and skills development
- Average training hours per employee per year
- % employees receiving regular performance and career development reviews
- Transition assistance programmes (e.g., reskilling for technology change)

### S1-14 — Health and safety (Quantitative)
- % employees covered by a health & safety management system (ISO 45001 or equivalent)
- Number of work-related fatalities (employees and non-employees)
- Lost Time Injury Frequency Rate (LTIFR) per 1 million hours worked
- Number of recordable work-related accidents
- Work-related ill health cases

### S1-15 — Work-life balance
- Parental leave uptake rates, broken down by gender

### S1-16 — Compensation metrics (Quantitative)
- CEO pay ratio (CEO total remuneration / median employee total remuneration)
- Gender pay gap (median remuneration women vs. men, expressed as %)

*Phase-in relief: The gender pay gap (S1-16) and some other S1 datapoints have phase-in provisions for first-year reporters. Confirm what phase-in applies to your first reporting year.*

## 4. ESRS G1 — Business Conduct (Material)

### G1-1 — Corporate culture and business conduct policies
- Code of conduct / code of ethics
- Anti-corruption and anti-bribery policies
- Lobbying and political engagement policies
- Payment practices policies (prompt payment to suppliers)

### G1-2 — Management of supplier relationships
- Responsible procurement policies
- Fair dealing with suppliers (payment terms, supplier code of conduct)
- Coverage of supply chain due diligence at tier-1 supplier level

### G1-3 — Prevention and detection of corruption and bribery
- Corruption risk assessment methodology
- Anti-corruption training coverage (% of relevant employees trained)
- Processes for detecting and reporting corruption

### G1-4 — Incidents of corruption or bribery (Quantitative)
- Number of confirmed corruption convictions during the reporting period
- Total monetary value of fines for corruption
- Actions taken in response to confirmed incidents

### G1-5 — Political influence and lobbying activities
- Total financial or in-kind contributions to political parties
- Description of significant lobbying activities and positions taken

### G1-6 — Payment practices (Quantitative)
- Average payment period to suppliers (days)
- % of payments made beyond contractual payment terms
- Description of complaints received from suppliers about payment practices

## 5. Materiality Statement for Non-Material Topics

For all topics assessed as non-material — ESRS E2 (Pollution), E3 (Water), E4 (Biodiversity), E5 (Circular Economy), S2 (Value Chain Workers), S3 (Communities), and S4 (Consumers) — you are not required to produce the full disclosures under those standards.

However, you **must** include in your ESRS 2 SBM-3 and IRO-1 disclosures:
- A brief explanation that each topic was assessed and found non-material
- The basis for that conclusion (impact materiality and financial materiality assessment)
- How stakeholders were engaged in reaching those conclusions

This materiality statement provides the audit trail for auditors and stakeholders to understand why those standards were omitted.

## 6. Format, Assurance, and Digital Tagging Requirements

### Location in annual report
All CSRD disclosures must appear in a **dedicated section of the management report** (Accounting Directive, Art. 19a). They cannot be published as a standalone sustainability report.

### Third-party assurance (Art. 26a CSRD)
- **Limited assurance** is required from your first reporting year
- Assurance must cover: compliance with ESRS, the DMA process, and the sustainability information disclosed
- Provided by your statutory auditor or an independent assurance services provider (IASP)
- **Reasonable assurance** will be phased in at a later date (Commission review by 2028)

### Digital tagging (XBRL/iXBRL)
All sustainability disclosures must be **digitally tagged** in XBRL/iXBRL format using the European Single Electronic Format (ESEF). The Commission's sustainability tagging taxonomy applies.

### Comparative data
Prior-year comparative data is required for all quantitative disclosures (ESRS 1, para. 86). Phase-in provisions may apply in the first reporting year.

## 7. Key Data Collection Priorities

Given your material topics, focus your data collection efforts on:

| Data Area | Standard | Typical Challenge |
|-----------|----------|------------------|
| Scope 1 & 2 GHG emissions | E1-6 | Metering gaps, emission factor selection |
| Scope 3 GHG emissions (all 15 categories) | E1-6 | Value chain data availability, spend-based vs. activity-based methods |
| Transition plan narrative | E1-1 | Requires board-level strategy decision, not just data |
| Physical and transition risk assessment | E1-9 | Scenario analysis capability |
| EU Taxonomy alignment (revenue, capex, opex) | E1 / ESRS 2 | Technical screening criteria assessment |
| Gender pay gap | S1-16 | HR system configuration, consistent methodology |
| CEO pay ratio | S1-16 | Median calculation across all employees |
| LTIFR and H&S metrics | S1-14 | Consistent global H&S data collection |
| Collective bargaining coverage | S1-8 | Country-level HR data |
| Anti-corruption incident data | G1-4 | Legal/compliance case tracking |
| Supplier payment data | G1-6 | ERP/finance system reporting |

## 8. Omnibus Proposal Caveat

The European Commission proposed simplifications in the **CSRD Omnibus Package** (February 2025), which may reduce the number of mandatory datapoints and narrow reporting scope. The legislative process was ongoing as of the date of this guidance. You should monitor the status of the Omnibus Package before finalising your disclosure framework, as some datapoints listed above may be modified or made voluntary.
