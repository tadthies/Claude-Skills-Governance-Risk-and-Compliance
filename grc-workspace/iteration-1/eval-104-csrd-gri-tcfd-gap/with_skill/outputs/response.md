# GRI + TCFD to CSRD/ESRS Transition: Gap Assessment

## Executive Summary

If you already report under GRI Standards and publish a TCFD report, you have a meaningful head start — but you should expect **moderate-to-significant incremental work** to reach full CSRD/ESRS compliance. The good news: GRI and TCFD together cover a large portion of the climate and social disclosures required by ESRS. The challenge lies in CSRD's mandatory legal structure, its double materiality requirement, extensive governance disclosures, value chain obligations, digital tagging, and third-party assurance — none of which GRI or TCFD mandates.

A realistic estimate for a large company with mature GRI/TCFD reporting: **12–18 months of structured implementation work** before first reporting, assuming you run it in parallel with business-as-usual ESG reporting.

---

## What GRI and TCFD Give You (Your Starting Assets)

**From GRI Standards:**
- A materiality-assessed topic structure that maps broadly to ESRS topical standards (E1–E5, S1–S4, G1)
- Existing data collection processes for many quantitative disclosures (GHG, energy, water, waste, workforce metrics)
- Stakeholder engagement methodology
- Narrative disclosures on policies, targets, and management approaches

**From TCFD:**
- Climate-related governance disclosures (board and management roles) — directly useful for ESRS E1 and ESRS 2 GOV disclosures
- Climate risk scenario analysis (physical and transition risks) — maps to ESRS E1 physical and transition risk disclosures
- Metrics and targets for GHG emissions (Scope 1, 2, and likely Scope 3 at category level)
- Strategy disclosures on climate resilience

**GRI interoperability:** ESRS 1 Appendix C explicitly maps ESRS datapoints to GRI disclosures. GRI reporters can use this mapping to identify existing disclosures that already satisfy ESRS requirements, rather than starting from scratch.

**TCFD interoperability:** ESRS E1 incorporates TCFD recommendations directly. If your TCFD report covers governance, strategy, risk management, and metrics/targets for climate, you have the core architecture for ESRS E1.

## Gap Assessment Table

| ESRS Area | Current State (GRI + TCFD) | Gap | Priority | Recommended Action |
|-----------|---------------------------|-----|----------|--------------------|
| **ESRS 2 — Double Materiality Assessment (DMA)** | GRI uses impact materiality; TCFD uses financial materiality | GRI only covers one lens; no formal combined DMA process exists | **Critical** | Conduct a formal DMA integrating both impact and financial materiality per ESRS 1 paras. 45–56; document and disclose the process under SBM-3 |
| **ESRS 2 — Governance (GOV-1 to GOV-5)** | TCFD covers board/management climate roles; GRI covers some governance topics | GOV disclosures are broader than TCFD: must cover ALL sustainability topics, incentive schemes (GOV-3), internal controls for sustainability (GOV-5), and due diligence statement (GOV-4) | **High** | Map existing TCFD governance narrative to GOV-1/GOV-2; build out GOV-3 (incentives), GOV-4 (due diligence linkage), GOV-5 (risk controls) for all ESRS topics |
| **ESRS 2 — Strategy & Business Model (SBM-1, SBM-2, SBM-3)** | GRI partial; TCFD partial for climate | SBM-1 requires value chain description; SBM-2 requires documented stakeholder engagement per topic; SBM-3 requires full IRO disclosure tied to DMA | **High** | Expand strategy disclosures beyond climate; formalize stakeholder engagement documentation; link DMA outputs to SBM-3 |
| **ESRS E1 — Climate Change** | Strong: TCFD covers scenario analysis, governance, strategy, metrics/targets; GRI covers Scope 1/2/3 | Likely gaps: Scope 3 all 15 categories (if not already complete), GHG intensity per net revenue, EU Taxonomy alignment disclosures, transition plan with financial planning (capex/opex for decarbonisation), locked-in assets | **High** | Complete Scope 3 category inventory; prepare EU Taxonomy eligibility and alignment calculation; draft ESRS-compliant transition plan per ESRS E1-1 |
| **ESRS E2 — Pollution** | GRI 305 (emissions) covers some; may have limited pollution disclosures | Likely a significant gap unless you are in a sector with heavy pollution relevance; specific ESRS E2 datapoints (substances of concern, pollution incidents, PRTR-linked disclosures) are more granular than typical GRI | **Medium** | Run DMA to determine materiality; if material, build data collection for air/water/soil pollutants and substances of concern |
| **ESRS E3 — Water & Marine Resources** | GRI 303 covers water withdrawal and consumption | GRI 303 aligns reasonably well; gap is likely in marine-specific disclosures and water stress area disaggregation | **Medium** | Cross-reference GRI 303 data to ESRS E3 datapoints; add water stress area breakdown if not already present |
| **ESRS E4 — Biodiversity & Ecosystems** | GRI 304 (biodiversity) is typically thin in most GRI reports | This is commonly the largest environmental gap for GRI reporters; ESRS E4 requires site-level biodiversity impact assessment, ecosystem services assessment, targets per the Kunming-Montreal framework | **High** | Assess biodiversity materiality in DMA; if material, engage specialist support for site-level assessment and ecosystem services mapping |
| **ESRS E5 — Resource Use & Circular Economy** | GRI 301 (materials), GRI 306 (waste) provide a base | ESRS E5 requires circular economy strategy disclosure and material flow data that goes beyond typical GRI waste disclosures | **Medium** | Map GRI 301/306 disclosures to ESRS E5 datapoints; identify gaps in circular economy strategy narrative |
| **ESRS S1 — Own Workforce** | GRI 400-series covers many workforce topics | Key gaps: gender pay gap disclosure (aligned with EU Pay Transparency Directive), collective bargaining coverage %, health & safety management system coverage, and more granular employee breakdown by country (for large companies) | **High** | Conduct ESRS S1 datapoint mapping against existing GRI 400-series data; build gender pay gap calculation and reporting process |
| **ESRS S2 — Workers in Value Chain** | GRI 408/409 cover some supply chain labour issues | ESRS S2 requires systematic value chain labour rights assessment — typically far more granular than GRI practice; data availability from suppliers is a common pain point | **High** | Map tier-1 supplier coverage; establish value chain data collection programme; use sector averages/proxies where direct data unavailable (disclose methodology) |
| **ESRS S3 — Affected Communities** | GRI 413 covers community impact | Typically a gap in formalised community IRO assessment and documentation tied to DMA | **Medium** | Assess materiality in DMA; if material, formalise community engagement and impact documentation |
| **ESRS S4 — Consumers & End-Users** | GRI 416/417/418 cover product safety and data | ESRS S4 requires consumer-related IRO process; may be covered adequately by GRI if disclosures are current | **Low–Medium** | Cross-reference existing GRI 416–418 disclosures to ESRS S4 datapoints |
| **ESRS G1 — Business Conduct** | GRI 205 (anti-corruption), GRI 206 (anti-competitive behaviour) provide a base | ESRS G1 adds payment practices (average payment days to suppliers), lobbying disclosure, and political contributions — often not in GRI reports | **Medium** | Add payment practices data collection; review lobbying and political contribution disclosures |
| **Reporting Location (Management Report)** | GRI report is typically standalone; TCFD may be in annual report | CSRD requires sustainability information in the management report (Accounting Directive Art. 19a), not as a standalone document | **High** | Work with legal/finance to integrate sustainability section into the annual management report; this requires coordination with auditors |
| **Digital Tagging (XBRL/iXBRL)** | Not applicable under GRI or TCFD | Full new requirement; all sustainability disclosures must be tagged using ESEF taxonomy (Commission sustainability taxonomy pending) | **High** | Engage reporting technology provider or work with finance team already using ESEF for financial statements; allow 3–6 months for taxonomy mapping and tagging setup |
| **Third-Party Assurance** | GRI assurance optional (many companies obtain it); TCFD no assurance requirement | CSRD requires limited assurance from statutory auditor or IASP from first reporting year (Art. 26a); this is a legal obligation, not optional | **Critical** | Engage statutory auditor or IASP now — assurance scope, readiness, and fees need to be agreed well before year-end; if you have GRI assurance, your provider may have a pathway |
| **Value Chain Data Collection** | GRI encourages but does not mandate systematic value chain data | CSRD requires value chain impacts, risks, and opportunities to be assessed where material; data from suppliers and downstream partners is needed | **High** | Run value chain mapping exercise tied to DMA; build supplier data requests into procurement contracts; begin with material topics only |

## Top Gaps to Prioritise

### 1. Double Materiality Assessment — Start Immediately
Your GRI materiality process covers only impact materiality. TCFD covers financial materiality for climate only. CSRD requires a combined DMA across all ESRS topics. This is the **foundation of your entire CSRD report** — without it, you cannot determine which topical standards apply or which datapoints to collect. Begin the DMA process at least 12 months before your first reporting year.

### 2. Assurance Readiness — Engage Your Auditor Now
Limited assurance is legally required from day one. Many statutory auditors are developing CSRD assurance methodologies. Engaging early allows you to understand what evidence they will require, which will drive your data governance and documentation approach. If you currently obtain GRI assurance, your existing provider may be well-positioned to extend scope.

### 3. Scope 3 Completeness and Value Chain Data
Most GRI reporters disclose Scope 3 selectively. ESRS E1 requires all 15 Scope 3 categories if climate is material, with clear disclosure of estimation methodology. Value chain social data (ESRS S2) is also a common and significant gap, requiring supplier engagement programmes.

### 4. Biodiversity (ESRS E4)
This is the most frequent "surprise" gap for GRI reporters. Unless you have already aligned with TNFD or published detailed biodiversity disclosures, expect to build this largely from scratch if it is material after your DMA.

### 5. Governance Integration and Incentives (ESRS 2 GOV)
TCFD governance disclosures cover climate. ESRS 2 GOV requires board and management oversight of all material sustainability topics, disclosure of sustainability-linked incentive schemes, and internal controls over sustainability reporting — significantly broader than most existing frameworks require.

### 6. Management Report Integration and XBRL Tagging
These are structural/legal requirements with no equivalent in GRI or TCFD. They require coordination across finance, legal, IT, and sustainability teams and typically take 6–12 months to implement properly.

## Realistic Work Estimate

| Phase | Timeline | Key Activities |
|-------|----------|---------------|
| **Scoping & DMA** | Months 1–4 | Confirm CSRD applicability, conduct DMA, identify material ESRS topics |
| **Gap Analysis** | Months 3–5 | Map existing GRI/TCFD disclosures to ESRS datapoints topic by topic |
| **Data Architecture** | Months 4–9 | Build data collection processes for gaps (value chain, pay gap, biodiversity, Scope 3 completeness) |
| **Governance & Policy** | Months 5–8 | Establish board oversight processes, sustainability governance documentation, due diligence statement |
| **Reporting Integration** | Months 7–11 | Draft ESRS disclosures, integrate into management report structure, prepare XBRL tagging |
| **Assurance Preparation** | Months 6–12 | Engage auditor, build evidence trail, conduct pre-assurance readiness review |
| **First CSRD Report Published** | End of Year 1 or 2 (depending on your scope category) | Full ESRS-compliant report with limited assurance |

## Key References

- **ESRS 1, Appendix C** — GRI-to-ESRS mapping (use this to identify which existing GRI disclosures satisfy ESRS datapoints)
- **ESRS 2, GOV-1 to GOV-5, SBM-1 to SBM-3, IRO-1** — mandatory for all in-scope companies regardless of DMA
- **ESRS E1** — incorporates TCFD recommendations; TCFD reporters have a strong foundation but gaps exist on EU Taxonomy, transition plan financial detail, and Scope 3 completeness
- **Art. 19a CSRD (Directive 2022/2464)** — management report location requirement
- **Art. 26a CSRD** — assurance obligation
- **Commission Delegated Regulation (EU) 2023/2772** — ESRS standards text

**Note on the Omnibus Proposal (February 2025):** The European Commission has proposed simplifications that may narrow the scope of CSRD and reduce mandatory datapoints, particularly for the second wave of reporters. Monitor the legislative status of this proposal, as it may affect which ESRS topical standards are mandatory versus voluntary for your reporting category. However, the core DMA, ESRS 2 general disclosures, assurance, and management report location requirements are unlikely to be removed.
