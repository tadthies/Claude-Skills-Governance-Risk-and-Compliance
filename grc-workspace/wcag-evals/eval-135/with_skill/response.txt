1. INTRODUCTION
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

ShopEasy is committed to ensuring digital accessibility for people with
disabilities. We continually improve the user experience for everyone and apply
relevant accessibility standards to achieve this goal.

This accessibility statement applies to the ShopEasy website and all web-based
services operated under the shopeasy.com domain (hereinafter "the Website").

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
2. CONFORMANCE STATUS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

The Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) define requirements for
designers and developers to improve accessibility for people with disabilities.
WCAG 2.1 defines three levels of conformance: Level A (minimum), Level AA
(enhanced), and Level AAA (highest). ShopEasy targets WCAG 2.1 Level AA
conformance.

CONFORMANCE CLAIM: ShopEasy PARTIALLY CONFORMS with WCAG 2.1 Level AA.

"Partially conforms" means that some parts of the content do not conform to
the accessibility standard. The specific non-conformances are identified in
Section 4 of this statement.

This conformance claim covers the ShopEasy website as a whole, including:
  - All product listing and detail pages
  - Shopping cart and checkout processes
  - Account registration and management
  - Search functionality
  - Customer support pages
  - Blog and editorial content
  - Embedded third-party widgets (where applicable)

Excluded from this claim:
  - Third-party content not under our direct control (e.g., external payment
    provider interfaces), where those providers maintain their own separate
    conformance claims.

4. KNOWN ACCESSIBILITY ISSUES (NON-CONFORMANCES)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

The following known issues represent areas where ShopEasy does not currently
meet WCAG 2.1 Level AA. We are actively working to resolve these.

----------------------------------------------------------------------
Issue 1: Inaccessible PDF Product Manuals
----------------------------------------------------------------------
  Affected content:
    Older PDF product manuals available for download on product detail pages.

  WCAG Success Criteria not met:
    - SC 1.1.1 Non-text Content (Level A): Some PDF documents contain images,
      diagrams, and non-text elements that lack appropriate text alternatives.
      Users relying on screen readers may not receive equivalent information.
    - SC 4.1.2 Name, Role, Value (Level A): Some older PDFs lack proper tag
      structure, form field labels, and programmatic attributes required for
      assistive technologies to interpret interactive elements and reading order
      correctly.

  Impact:
    Users who rely on screen readers or other assistive technologies may be
    unable to access the full content of affected PDF manuals.

  Accessible alternative:
    If you require an accessible version of a specific product manual, please
    contact our accessibility support team (see Section 6). We will provide an
    accessible alternative in HTML or plain-text format within 5 business days.

  Remediation plan:
    We are systematically remediating all product manuals. New manuals published
    after 1 January 2025 are produced to WCAG 2.1 AA standards. Remediation of
    legacy documents is ongoing, with an estimated completion date of
    [INSERT TARGET DATE].

----------------------------------------------------------------------
Issue 2: Live Chat Widget Does Not Announce New Messages
----------------------------------------------------------------------
  Affected content:
    The live chat widget available site-wide via the chat icon in the lower
    right corner of the screen.

  WCAG Success Criteria not met:
    - SC 4.1.3 Status Messages (Level AA): When a new chat message arrives from
      a support agent, the message is displayed visually but not communicated
      to screen reader users via an appropriate ARIA live region or equivalent
      programmatic mechanism. Users of screen readers may therefore miss
      incoming messages without switching focus manually to the chat panel.

  Impact:
    Screen reader users who initiate a live chat session may not be aware that
    a support agent has responded, resulting in a degraded or unusable support
    experience.

  Accessible alternative:
    Screen reader users who experience difficulty with the live chat widget are
    encouraged to contact our support team by email or telephone instead
    (see Section 6).

  Remediation plan:
    We are working with our live chat widget provider to implement ARIA live
    region announcements (role="log" or aria-live="polite") for incoming
    messages. This fix is targeted for completion by [INSERT TARGET DATE].

----------------------------------------------------------------------
Issue 3: Video Product Demonstrations Lack Audio Descriptions
----------------------------------------------------------------------
  Affected content:
    Embedded product demonstration videos on product detail pages and in the
    ShopEasy Learning Hub.

  WCAG Success Criteria not met:
    - SC 1.2.5 Audio Description (Prerecorded) (Level AA): Video product
      demonstrations contain visual information (e.g., on-screen text, product
      features shown only visually, button interactions) that is not conveyed
      through the existing audio track. Extended or inserted audio descriptions
      are not currently provided for any prerecorded video content.

  Impact:
    Users who are blind or have low vision and cannot perceive the visual
    content of product videos may be unable to obtain equivalent information
    about product features and usage.

  Accessible alternative:
    We are preparing text transcripts for all product demonstration videos.
    Where transcripts are currently available, they are linked directly below
    the video player. If no transcript is yet available for a specific video,
    please contact us (see Section 6) and we will provide a descriptive
    transcript within 5 business days.

  Remediation plan:
    Audio descriptions are being produced for all new product videos (effective
    immediately). Existing video library remediation is in progress, prioritised
    by product popularity, with an estimated completion date of
    [INSERT TARGET DATE].

7. ENFORCEMENT AND COMPLAINTS PROCEDURE
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

We are committed to resolving all accessibility complaints. If you are
dissatisfied with our response to your accessibility feedback, you may escalate
your complaint as follows:

  Internal escalation:
    Email our Data Protection and Compliance team at: compliance@shopeasy.com
    Reference "Accessibility Complaint" in the subject line.

  External enforcement bodies (by jurisdiction):

  United Kingdom:
    Equality and Human Rights Commission (EHRC)
    Website: www.equalityhumanrights.com
    The Equality Act 2010 and the Public Sector Bodies (Websites and Mobile
    Applications) Accessibility Regulations 2018 apply to public sector bodies.
    Private sector organisations are subject to the Equality Act 2010.

  European Union:
    If ShopEasy operates in an EU member state as a public sector body or
    within scope of the EU Web Accessibility Directive (Directive 2016/2102),
    complaints may be directed to the relevant national enforcement body.
    For private sector e-commerce entities, the European Accessibility Act
    (Directive 2019/882), applicable from 28 June 2025, establishes
    accessibility requirements and member-state enforcement mechanisms.
    Contact the national market surveillance authority in your country of
    residence for complaints under the EAA.

  United States:
    The Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA) prohibits discrimination on the
    basis of disability. Complaints may be filed with the U.S. Department of
    Justice, Civil Rights Division: www.ada.gov

  Australia:
    Disability Discrimination Act 1992 complaints may be lodged with the
    Australian Human Rights Commission: www.humanrights.gov.au

9. COMPATIBILITY WITH BROWSERS AND ASSISTIVE TECHNOLOGIES
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

ShopEasy is designed to be compatible with the following assistive technologies
and browser combinations:

  Supported combinations (tested):
    - NVDA (latest release) + Mozilla Firefox (latest) on Windows 10/11
    - VoiceOver + Safari (latest) on macOS (latest two major versions)
    - VoiceOver + Safari on iOS (latest two major versions)
    - Keyboard-only navigation in Chrome, Firefox, Edge (latest versions)
    - TalkBack + Chrome on Android (latest two major versions)

  The Website is NOT optimised for use with Internet Explorer 11 or earlier
  versions of any browser. Users are encouraged to use a modern, supported
  browser for the best accessible experience.
