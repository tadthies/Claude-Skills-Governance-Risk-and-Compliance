WCAG Legal Compliance Requirements: US, EU, and UK Jurisdictions
For a SaaS Company Serving Government and Private Sector Customers
Date: 2026-05-16
================================================================================

EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
-----------------
As a SaaS company with customers in the US, EU, and UK spanning both government
agencies and private sector businesses, you face overlapping accessibility
mandates. The practical target that satisfies all jurisdictions is:

    WCAG 2.1 Level AA

This is the lowest common denominator that satisfies every applicable law
across your customer base. Where WCAG 2.2 is emerging as a requirement, the
good news is that WCAG 2.2 AA is a superset of WCAG 2.1 AA — achieving 2.2 AA
automatically satisfies 2.1 AA requirements. Targeting WCAG 2.2 AA now future-
proofs your product.

================================================================================
1. UNITED STATES — FEDERAL GOVERNMENT CUSTOMERS
================================================================================

Applicable Law: Section 508 of the Rehabilitation Act of 1973
(29 U.S.C. § 794d), as amended and refreshed in 2018

Regulatory Body: U.S. Access Board; enforced via agency procurement and
the DOJ under the ADA for federally funded entities.

WCAG Requirement: WCAG 2.0 Level AA (incorporated by reference)

Details:
- The 2017 Section 508 "Refresh" (effective January 18, 2018) replaced the
  original 2001 technical standards by incorporating WCAG 2.0 Level AA by
  reference (36 C.F.R. Part 1194).
- The standard references WCAG 2.0, not 2.1 or 2.2 — however, the Access
  Board has signaled intent to update the reference to WCAG 2.1 or later.
  As of 2026, no formal rulemaking has finalized that update, so WCAG 2.0
  Level AA remains the legally referenced standard.
- In practice, agencies increasingly require or prefer WCAG 2.1 AA in
  solicitations and ACR (Accessibility Conformance Report / VPAT) reviews.
- Applies to: Electronic and Information Technology (EIT) procured by
  federal agencies. As a SaaS vendor to federal agencies, your product is
  subject to Section 508 review during procurement.
- You must provide an Accessibility Conformance Report (ACR) using the
  VPAT 2.x template (ITI VPAT) referencing WCAG 2.1 — this is the current
  industry-standard VPAT revision even though the regulation cites 2.0.

Key Deadlines:
- The 2017 refresh took effect January 18, 2018; no pending phase-in remains.
- Watch for a future rulemaking to update the WCAG reference — likely to 2.2.

Action Required: Achieve WCAG 2.0 Level AA (minimum legal), document via
VPAT 2.x referencing WCAG 2.1 Level AA for practical market access.

2. UNITED STATES — PRIVATE SECTOR CUSTOMERS
================================================================================

Applicable Laws:
  (a) Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA), Titles II and III
      (42 U.S.C. §§ 12101 et seq.)
  (b) DOJ Final Rule on Web Accessibility for Title II entities
      (28 C.F.R. Part 35, published April 24, 2024)
  (c) Air Carrier Access Act (ACAA) — if applicable to airline customers
  (d) Section 504 of the Rehabilitation Act — for federally funded recipients

WCAG Requirement:
  - Title II (state/local government): WCAG 2.1 Level AA — NOW MANDATED
  - Title III (private businesses): WCAG 2.1 Level AA — DOJ guidance and
    overwhelming court precedent treat 2.1 AA as the de facto standard;
    no formal final rule yet but litigation risk is high without it.

Title II DOJ Final Rule (April 2024) Details:
- The DOJ finalized a rule under ADA Title II (28 C.F.R. Part 35) requiring
  state and local government entities to conform to WCAG 2.1 Level AA for
  web content and mobile apps.
- Phase-in deadlines by entity size:
    * Population 50,000+: Compliance by April 24, 2026
    * Population 10,000–49,999: Compliance by April 26, 2027
    * Population under 10,000: Compliance by April 26, 2027
- NOTE: As of May 2026, the initial deadline for large entities has passed.
  If your customers include state/local government entities, they must now
  be compliant — and as their SaaS provider, your product must support their
  compliance.

Title III (Private Sector):
- No equivalent DOJ final rule exists for private businesses as of 2026, but
  the DOJ's 2022 guidance letter and consistent federal court decisions
  (9th Cir., 1st Cir., 11th Cir.) treat WCAG 2.1 AA as the appropriate
  technical standard.
- ADA Title III litigation volume remains high; WCAG 2.1 AA is the practical
  safe harbor even without a formal rule.
- State laws (e.g., California Unruh Act, New York state/city laws) may impose
  additional or parallel obligations.

Action Required: WCAG 2.1 Level AA is both the legal minimum for Title II
customers (mandated) and the prudent standard for all private sector customers.

3. EUROPEAN UNION — PUBLIC SECTOR CUSTOMERS
================================================================================

Applicable Law: EU Web Accessibility Directive
(Directive (EU) 2016/2102, "WAD")

Harmonized Standard: EN 301 549 V3.2.1 (2021) — incorporates WCAG 2.1 Level AA

WCAG Requirement: WCAG 2.1 Level AA (via EN 301 549)

Details:
- The WAD requires public sector bodies in all EU Member States to make their
  websites and mobile apps accessible in accordance with EN 301 549.
- EN 301 549 V3.2.1 (current as of 2026) incorporates WCAG 2.1 Level AA
  as its core web content requirements (Chapter 9 maps directly to WCAG 2.1).
- As a SaaS vendor to public sector bodies, your product must conform to
  EN 301 549 / WCAG 2.1 AA to be procurable under WAD-compliant procurement.
- Scope: All public sector bodies (government, universities, hospitals,
  publicly funded entities). Does NOT currently mandate private sector
  compliance under the WAD — that is covered by the EAA (see Section 4).
- Accessibility Statement: Required for public sector websites/apps; as a
  SaaS provider you may need to support your customers in generating these.

Key Deadlines: WAD has been in force since September 23, 2020 (websites) and
June 23, 2021 (mobile apps). All deadlines have passed.

EN 301 549 Updates: ETSI is expected to update EN 301 549 to reference
WCAG 2.2 in an upcoming revision. Monitor ETSI TC HF for publication.

Action Required: WCAG 2.1 Level AA conformance, documented via an
Accessibility Conformance Report against EN 301 549 V3.2.1.

4. EUROPEAN UNION — PRIVATE SECTOR CUSTOMERS
================================================================================

Applicable Law: European Accessibility Act
(Directive (EU) 2019/882, "EAA")

Harmonized Standard: EN 301 549 (same standard as WAD, incorporating WCAG 2.1 AA)

WCAG Requirement: WCAG 2.1 Level AA (via EN 301 549, per EAA)

Details:
- The EAA extends accessibility requirements to a broad range of private sector
  products and services, including: computers, smartphones, e-readers,
  e-commerce websites, banking services, e-books, audio/visual media services,
  transport services (ticketing/check-in), and — critically for SaaS —
  electronic communications services.
- "Services" in scope include any digital service provided to consumers in the
  EU. B2B SaaS is more nuanced: the EAA primarily targets B2C services, but
  if your SaaS is used to deliver services to end consumers (e.g., a customer-
  facing portal your EU enterprise customers use to serve their own customers),
  EAA obligations likely flow through.
- Member States were required to transpose the EAA into national law by
  June 28, 2022.

KEY DEADLINE — EAA COMPLIANCE DATE: June 28, 2025
- All covered products and services placed on the market or provided in the
  EU after June 28, 2025 must comply with EAA requirements.
- This deadline has now passed (as of May 2026). If your SaaS is in scope,
  EAA compliance is currently required.

Microenterprise Exemption:
- Microenterprises (fewer than 10 employees AND annual turnover/balance sheet
  not exceeding €2 million) providing services (not products) are exempt from
  the EAA. This exemption does not apply to products.

Disproportionate Burden Exception:
- Economic operators may claim an exemption if compliance would impose a
  disproportionate burden, but this requires documented assessment and is
  a narrow exception.

Action Required: Assess whether your SaaS services fall within EAA scope.
If yes (likely for B2C-facing functionality), WCAG 2.1 Level AA conformance
is required as of June 28, 2025.

5. UNITED KINGDOM
================================================================================

Applicable Laws:
  (a) Public Sector Bodies (Websites and Mobile Applications) Accessibility
      Regulations 2018 (SI 2018/952) — "UK PSBAR" — for public sector customers
  (b) Equality Act 2010 — for private sector customers
  (c) (Prospective) UK Accessibility Act / adoption of EAA equivalent —
      legislation in progress as of 2026

Public Sector (UK PSBAR):
WCAG Requirement: WCAG 2.1 Level AA

Details:
- UK PSBAR transposed the EU WAD into UK law pre-Brexit and was retained
  in domestic law post-Brexit.
- References EN 301 549 / WCAG 2.1 Level AA as the technical standard.
- Enforced by the Government Digital Service (GDS) / Cabinet Office.
- Applies to public sector bodies in England, Wales, Scotland (separate
  Scottish regulations apply), and Northern Ireland.
- Requires accessibility statements for covered websites and apps.
- Deadlines have passed: websites (September 23, 2020), mobile apps
  (June 23, 2021).

Private Sector (Equality Act 2010):
WCAG Requirement: No specific version mandated, but WCAG 2.1 AA is the
prevailing standard in UK case law and regulatory guidance.

Details:
- The Equality Act 2010 (sections 20–21) imposes a duty on service providers
  to make "reasonable adjustments" to ensure disabled people are not at a
  substantial disadvantage.
- The Equality and Human Rights Commission (EHRC) Code of Practice identifies
  website/digital accessibility as a key reasonable adjustment area.
- WCAG 2.1 Level AA is the de facto standard expected by UK courts and the
  EHRC, even without explicit statutory reference.
- UK has signaled intent to introduce EAA-equivalent private sector
  requirements post-Brexit, but as of May 2026 no equivalent legislation
  has been enacted for private businesses. Monitor UK government consultations.

Action Required: WCAG 2.1 Level AA for public sector customers (legally
mandated); WCAG 2.1 Level AA recommended for private sector customers
(Equality Act reasonable adjustment standard).

8. PRACTICAL RECOMMENDATIONS
================================================================================

TARGET STANDARD: WCAG 2.2 Level AA
--------------------------------------
While WCAG 2.1 AA is the legal minimum across all jurisdictions, we strongly
recommend targeting WCAG 2.2 Level AA for the following reasons:

1. WCAG 2.2 is a superset of 2.1 — achieving 2.2 AA automatically satisfies
   all 2.1 AA requirements.
2. EN 301 549 is expected to reference WCAG 2.2 in its next revision,
   making 2.2 the future EU standard.
3. The US Access Board is expected to update Section 508 to reference WCAG 2.1
   or 2.2 in future rulemaking.
4. WCAG 2.2 adds 9 new success criteria (SC 2.4.11–2.4.13, 2.5.7–2.5.8,
   3.2.6, 3.3.7–3.3.9) that significantly improve mobile and cognitive
   accessibility — areas of increasing legal focus.
5. Future-proofing reduces remediation costs later.

STEP-BY-STEP COMPLIANCE ROADMAP:
----------------------------------

Step 1 — Baseline Audit (Immediate)
  - Conduct a WCAG 2.2 Level AA audit of your entire product (web + mobile).
  - Use automated tools (axe-core, Deque, WAVE) combined with manual testing
    and testing with assistive technology users (screen readers, keyboard-only,
    voice control).
  - Prioritize critical user journeys: sign-up, login, core product features,
    billing, support.

Step 2 — Remediation Backlog (0–6 months)
  - Triage findings by impact (blocker vs. moderate) and user flow criticality.
  - Fix blockers (keyboard traps, missing alt text, no focus indicators,
    inaccessible forms, missing error identification) first.
  - Establish a Definition of Done that includes accessibility for all new
    features going forward.

Step 3 — Documentation (Parallel)
  - Produce a VPAT 2.x (ACR) against WCAG 2.1 AA for US government procurement.
  - Produce an EN 301 549 V3.2.1 conformance report for EU/UK procurement.
  - Publish an Accessibility Statement on your website covering your current
    status, known issues, and contact mechanism for assistance requests.

Step 4 — Ongoing Testing Program
  - Integrate automated accessibility testing into your CI/CD pipeline.
  - Schedule quarterly manual audits covering assistive technology combinations:
    * NVDA + Firefox (Windows)
    * JAWS + Chrome (Windows)
    * VoiceOver + Safari (macOS/iOS)
    * TalkBack + Chrome (Android)
  - Update your ACR/VPAT at least annually or after major releases.

Step 5 — Legal Safeguards
  - Include accessibility commitments in customer contracts (SLA provisions).
  - Establish an accessibility feedback/complaint mechanism — required under
    WAD (EU) and PSBAR (UK), and best practice under ADA.
  - Document your remediation roadmap — demonstrates good faith under ADA
    undue hardship analysis and EAA disproportionate burden exception.

ARIA AND TECHNICAL NOTES:
  - Follow WAI-ARIA Authoring Practices Guide (APG) for all custom widgets.
  - Ensure all custom components have appropriate ARIA roles, states, and
    properties before relying on ARIA (use native HTML semantics first).
  - Test focus management for single-page application (SPA) route changes —
    a common failure in SaaS products.
  - Ensure all dynamic content updates are announced via live regions
    (aria-live, aria-atomic) appropriately.

9. SPECIFIC WCAG 2.2 SUCCESS CRITERIA TO PRIORITIZE (COMMON SAAS FAILURES)
================================================================================

The following SCs are frequently failed in SaaS applications and should receive
immediate attention during audit:

WCAG 2.1 (often failed in SaaS):
  1.4.3  Contrast (Minimum) — text/background color contrast ≥ 4.5:1
  1.4.4  Resize Text — usable at 200% zoom without loss of content
  1.4.11 Non-text Contrast — UI components/graphics ≥ 3:1 contrast ratio
  1.4.12 Text Spacing — content doesn't break with letter/line/word spacing overrides
  1.4.13 Content on Hover or Focus — tooltips/popovers are dismissible, hoverable, persistent
  2.1.1  Keyboard — all functionality available via keyboard
  2.4.3  Focus Order — logical focus sequence in modals and overlays
  2.4.7  Focus Visible — keyboard focus indicator always visible
  3.3.1  Error Identification — errors described in text, not color alone
  3.3.2  Labels or Instructions — form inputs have visible labels
  4.1.2  Name, Role, Value — all UI components have accessible name/role/state
  4.1.3  Status Messages — non-focused status messages announced to screen readers

WCAG 2.2 additions (new requirements):
  2.4.11 Focus Appearance (Minimum) — focus indicator meets size and contrast requirements
  2.5.7  Dragging Movements — all drag-and-drop has a single-pointer alternative
  2.5.8  Target Size (Minimum) — interactive targets ≥ 24×24 CSS pixels
  3.2.6  Consistent Help — help mechanisms appear in consistent location
  3.3.7  Redundant Entry — previously entered information is auto-populated
  3.3.8  Accessible Authentication (Minimum) — no cognitive function test for login
         (this directly impacts CAPTCHA-based authentication flows)
