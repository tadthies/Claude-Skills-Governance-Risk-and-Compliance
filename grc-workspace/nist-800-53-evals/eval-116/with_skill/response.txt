NIST SP 800-53 Rev 5 COMPLIANCE ADVISORY RESPONSE
FIPS 199 Categorization & Baseline Selection — Federal HR System
================================================================================

SYSTEM DESCRIPTION
------------------
System Name:    Federal Human Resources Information System (HRIS)
System Type:    Internal agency application (on-premises data center)
User Population: 5,000 federal employees
Data Types:     Personally Identifiable Information (PII), salary/compensation
                data, performance evaluation records
Hosting:        Internal government data center (not cloud)

================================================================================
STEP 1 — FIPS 199 SYSTEM CATEGORIZATION
================================================================================

Authority: Federal Information Processing Standard (FIPS) Publication 199,
"Standards for Security Categorization of Federal Information and Information
Systems" (February 2004), implemented under FISMA 2014 (44 U.S.C. § 3551).

Methodology: Each information type processed by the system is assessed against
the three security objectives — Confidentiality, Integrity, and Availability —
and assigned a potential impact level of Low, Moderate, or High. The overall
system categorization is determined by the high-water mark across all three
objectives for all information types in scope.

SP 800-60 Reference: NIST SP 800-60 Volume II, "Guide for Mapping Types of
Information and Information Systems to Security Categories," provides the
recommended impact levels for standard government information types. The
following mappings apply to this HR system:

INFORMATION TYPES IN SCOPE
---------------------------
1. Employee PII (names, SSNs, addresses, dates of birth, contact info)
   SP 800-60 Type: C.3.1.1 — General Personnel Records / Privacy Data

2. Salary and Compensation Data (pay grades, salaries, benefits, deductions)
   SP 800-60 Type: C.3.1.2 — Payroll and Benefits Data

3. Performance Review Records (ratings, disciplinary actions, promotion records)
   SP 800-60 Type: C.3.1.1 — General Personnel Records

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
CIA IMPACT ASSESSMENT — PER INFORMATION TYPE
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

INFORMATION TYPE 1: Employee PII (5,000 staff records)
-------------------------------------------------------
CONFIDENTIALITY — MODERATE
  Rationale: Unauthorized disclosure of PII for 5,000 federal employees would
  cause serious adverse effects to those individuals (identity theft risk,
  financial harm, reputational damage) and to the agency (regulatory sanctions,
  loss of public trust, notification obligations under the Privacy Act of 1974).
  A breach would not rise to the level of "severe or catastrophic" affecting
  national security or critical operations, but the scale (5,000 records) and
  sensitivity of SSNs and financial data clearly exceeds a "limited" impact.
  Per SP 800-60, PII data is typically assigned Moderate Confidentiality.
  Impact Level: MODERATE

INTEGRITY — MODERATE
  Rationale: Unauthorized modification of employee PII records (e.g., altering
  direct deposit routing numbers, changing addresses, falsifying identity
  fields) would cause serious adverse effects — incorrect payroll disbursements,
  inability to locate employees in emergencies, erroneous background-check
  results. While damaging, such modifications are generally detectable and
  correctable; they do not rise to catastrophic operational failure.
  Impact Level: MODERATE

AVAILABILITY — LOW
  Rationale: Temporary unavailability of general PII records (beyond active
  payroll functions) would have limited adverse effect. Employees can be
  identified through alternative records; short-term outages do not prevent
  mission execution. HR lookups are important but not time-critical at the
  operational level.
  Impact Level: LOW

INFORMATION TYPE 2: Salary and Compensation Data
-------------------------------------------------
CONFIDENTIALITY — MODERATE
  Rationale: Federal salary data for GS-scale employees is partially public
  information under OPM regulations, but specific compensation details
  (locality pay, benefits elections, deductions, garnishments) are protected
  under the Privacy Act. Unauthorized disclosure would cause serious adverse
  effects to individual employees and could expose the agency to Privacy Act
  violations. Scale of 5,000 records elevates impact beyond Low.
  Impact Level: MODERATE

INTEGRITY — MODERATE
  Rationale: Integrity of payroll-linked salary data is critical. Unauthorized
  modification could result in improper payments, violations of the Improper
  Payments Information Act, OPM audit findings, and financial harm to employees.
  Errors in salary records could propagate into payroll processing systems,
  causing systematic overpayments or underpayments. Recovery could be complex
  and time-consuming, constituting a serious adverse effect.
  Impact Level: MODERATE

AVAILABILITY — MODERATE
  Rationale: Salary/compensation data feeds into payroll processing cycles. If
  the HR system is unavailable during a pay period processing window, payroll
  for 5,000 employees cannot be processed on time — a serious adverse effect
  on both employees and agency operations. Payroll deadlines are immovable;
  a missed pay cycle constitutes significant operational and legal harm.
  Impact Level: MODERATE

INFORMATION TYPE 3: Performance Review Records
-----------------------------------------------
CONFIDENTIALITY — MODERATE
  Rationale: Performance evaluations, disciplinary records, and promotion
  decisions are sensitive personnel data protected under the Privacy Act and
  5 U.S.C. § 552a. Disclosure could harm employee reputations, expose the
  agency to grievance proceedings, and undermine trust in the HR process.
  Unauthorized access to these records for 5,000 employees constitutes a
  serious adverse effect.
  Impact Level: MODERATE

INTEGRITY — MODERATE
  Rationale: Tampering with performance ratings or disciplinary records would
  have serious adverse effects — improper promotions, wrongful disciplinary
  actions, civil service merit system violations, and potential litigation
  (whistleblower retaliation, EEO claims). The integrity of the federal merit
  system is foundational to civil service law.
  Impact Level: MODERATE

AVAILABILITY — LOW
  Rationale: Performance reviews are typically accessed infrequently (annual
  review cycles, promotion boards). Temporary unavailability has limited adverse
  effect on day-to-day agency operations. Review cycles can be rescheduled in
  most circumstances.
  Impact Level: LOW

STEP 2 — NIST SP 800-53B BASELINE SELECTION
================================================================================

Authority: NIST SP 800-53B, "Control Baselines for Information Systems and
Organizations" (October 2020).

SELECTED BASELINE: MODERATE

Rationale:
  The FIPS 199 high-water mark of MODERATE maps directly to the SP 800-53B
  Moderate control baseline. This baseline encompasses approximately 323
  controls and control enhancements and is the appropriate starting point for
  all federal information systems categorized at the Moderate impact level.

  The Moderate baseline is also the most common baseline applied to federal
  administrative and mission-support systems, and it aligns with FedRAMP
  Moderate authorization requirements for cloud equivalents of systems of
  this type.

Why NOT Low:
  The Low baseline is insufficient for a system storing PII for 5,000
  employees, salary data with payroll dependencies, and sensitive performance
  records. The Privacy Act, OMB Circular A-130, and the NIST Privacy Framework
  all require controls commensurate with the sensitivity and volume of PII
  processed. Low baseline controls do not provide adequate access controls,
  audit capabilities, or data protection measures for this data sensitivity.

Why NOT High:
  A High categorization would be appropriate if a breach would cause severe or
  catastrophic harm — such as loss of life, national security compromise, or
  widespread critical infrastructure failure. While unauthorized disclosure of
  5,000 employees' PII is serious, it does not meet the FIPS 199 threshold for
  "severe or catastrophic" adverse effects. High baseline controls would impose
  disproportionate burden without proportionate risk reduction for this system.
  If future analysis reveals that the system supports law enforcement,
  intelligence, or national security HR functions, High should be reconsidered.

MODERATE BASELINE — KEY CONTROL FAMILIES APPLICABLE TO THIS SYSTEM
-------------------------------------------------------------------

AC — Access Control (Moderate Baseline)
  AC-2    Account Management: Formal account creation, modification, removal
          and review procedures for all HR system users.
  AC-2(1) Automated System Account Management [M enhancement]
  AC-3    Access Enforcement: Role-based access control (RBAC) enforcing
          least privilege — HR staff see only records within their scope.
  AC-5    Separation of Duties: No single user can both create and approve
          salary changes.
  AC-6    Least Privilege
  AC-6(1) Authorize Access to Security Functions [M enhancement]
  AC-17   Remote Access: Controls for any VPN or remote HR system access.
  Recommended ODV: AC-2(3) — disable inactive accounts after 90 days.

AU — Audit and Accountability (Moderate Baseline)
  AU-2    Event Logging: Log all read/write/delete access to PII and salary
          records, privileged user actions, authentication events.
  AU-3    Content of Audit Records
  AU-9    Protection of Audit Information
  AU-11   Audit Record Retention
  Recommended ODV: AU-11 — retain audit logs for 3 years (aligned with
          Privacy Act records retention schedules).

IA — Identification and Authentication (Moderate Baseline)
  IA-2    Identification and Authentication (Org Users)
  IA-2(1) MFA for Privileged Accounts [M]: Multi-factor authentication
          required for all HR administrators and privileged roles.
  IA-2(2) MFA for Non-Privileged Accounts [M]: MFA recommended for all
          users given PII sensitivity.
  IA-5    Authenticator Management: Password complexity and rotation policies.
  IA-5(1) Password-Based Authentication [M enhancement]

SC — System and Communications Protection (Moderate Baseline)
  SC-8    Transmission Confidentiality and Integrity: Encrypt all data in
          transit (TLS 1.2+ minimum).
  SC-8(1) Cryptographic Protection [M enhancement]
  SC-28   Protection of Information at Rest: Encrypt PII, salary, and
          performance data at rest (AES-256 recommended).
  SC-28(1) Cryptographic Protection [M enhancement]
  SC-7    Boundary Protection: Network segmentation isolating the HR
          system from general-purpose networks.

SI — System and Information Integrity (Moderate Baseline)
  SI-2    Flaw Remediation: Patch critical vulnerabilities within 30 days;
          high within 60 days.
  SI-3    Malware Protection
  SI-7    Software, Firmware, and Information Integrity [M]
  SI-12   Information Management and Retention: Manage PII in accordance
          with Privacy Act records schedules.

CP — Contingency Planning (Moderate Baseline)
  CP-9    Information System Backup: Regular backups of all HR data.
  CP-9(1) Testing for Reliability/Integrity [M]
  CP-10   Information System Recovery and Reconstitution

RA — Risk Assessment (Moderate Baseline)
  RA-3    Risk Assessment: Conduct and document a system-level risk assessment.
  RA-5    Vulnerability Monitoring and Scanning: Regular vulnerability scans
          of the HR system and its hosting infrastructure.
  RA-3(1) Supply Chain Risk Assessment [M enhancement]: Assess risks from
          any third-party HR software components or vendors.

CA — Assessment, Authorization and Monitoring (Moderate Baseline)
  CA-2    Control Assessments: Independent security assessment before ATO.
  CA-7    Continuous Monitoring: Ongoing assessment of control effectiveness.
  CA-9    Internal System Connections: Document and authorize connections to
          payroll or other agency systems.

PS — Personnel Security (Moderate Baseline)
  PS-3    Personnel Screening: Background investigations for all HR system
          users commensurate with data sensitivity.
  PS-4    Personnel Termination: Immediate account deactivation upon departure.
  PS-5    Personnel Transfer: Modify access rights upon internal transfers.

PE — Physical and Environmental Protection (Moderate Baseline)
  PE-2    Physical Access Authorizations
  PE-3    Physical Access Control: Physical access controls for the data
          center hosting the HR system.
  PE-6    Monitoring Physical Access
  Note: As an on-premises data center deployment, PE controls are system-
  specific and cannot be fully inherited from a cloud provider.

STEP 3 — PT FAMILY (PII PROCESSING AND TRANSPARENCY) — MANDATORY OVERLAY
================================================================================

Because this system processes Personally Identifiable Information for 5,000
federal employees, the Privacy controls (PT family) under SP 800-53 Rev 5 are
mandatory regardless of the Moderate impact categorization. OMB Circular A-130,
"Managing Information as a Strategic Resource" (July 2016), requires agencies
to implement privacy controls for all systems processing PII.

The PT family (PT-1 through PT-8) must be applied as a privacy overlay on top
of the Moderate security baseline:

PT-1  — Policy and Procedures: Establish and maintain a PII processing policy
        documenting the legal authority, purpose, and use limitations for
        collecting employee PII.

PT-2  — Authority to Process PII: Document the legal authority authorizing the
        collection and use of employee PII (e.g., 5 U.S.C. Chapter 31 federal
        employment statutes, Privacy Act of 1974).

PT-3  — Purposes of Processing PII: Document and limit PII processing to
        stated HR purposes only. Do not use salary or performance data for
        unapproved secondary purposes.

PT-4  — Consent: Determine whether and how employee consent is required or
        appropriate for specific PII processing activities (note: employment
        relationships may not require separate consent for core HR functions,
        but supplemental uses require analysis).

PT-5  — Privacy Notice: Provide Privacy Act Statements at the point of PII
        collection informing employees of the authority, purpose, routine uses,
        and whether disclosure is mandatory or voluntary.
        Action Required: Publish a System of Records Notice (SORN) in the
        Federal Register for this HR system per Privacy Act requirements.

PT-6  — Privacy Reports: Report on PII processing activities as required by
        OMB reporting requirements.

PT-7  — Specific Categories of PII: Identify whether any special categories
        of PII are processed (e.g., financial account data for direct deposit,
        medical information in benefits records). Apply enhanced protections
        if sensitive PII categories are present.

PT-8  — Computer Matching Requirements: Assess whether any data matching
        with other federal agency records occurs, triggering Computer Matching
        and Privacy Protection Act requirements.

Additional Privacy Requirements:
  - Conduct a Privacy Impact Assessment (PIA) per E-Government Act of 2002
    Section 208 prior to system deployment.
  - Publish or update the System of Records Notice (SORN) per 5 U.S.C. § 552a.
  - Designate a Senior Agency Official for Privacy (SAOP) point of contact.
  - Document data minimization practices — collect only PII necessary for HR
    functions; do not aggregate beyond operational need.

STEP 4 — SP 800-60 REFERENCE DETAIL
================================================================================

NIST SP 800-60 Volume II provides the following specific guidance for the
information types present in this HR system:

Information Type C.3.1.1 — Personnel Records:
  Default Impact Values: Confidentiality = Moderate, Integrity = Moderate,
                         Availability = Low
  Adjustment Factors: The volume of records (5,000) and the inclusion of
  financial data (salary) supports maintaining Moderate ratings without
  downgrade.

Information Type C.3.1.2 — Payroll and Benefits Data:
  Default Impact Values: Confidentiality = Moderate, Integrity = Moderate,
                         Availability = Moderate
  Rationale: Payroll availability is time-sensitive due to pay period cycles;
  Moderate availability is appropriate and consistent with SP 800-60 defaults.

Practitioner Note: SP 800-60 default values may be adjusted up or down based
on specific agency mission context. Adjustments must be documented and approved
by the Authorizing Official (AO). In this case, no adjustments from the SP
800-60 defaults are warranted — the defaults match the mission risk analysis.

STEP 5 — RMF INTEGRATION PATH
================================================================================

This categorization decision feeds directly into the NIST Risk Management
Framework (RMF), SP 800-37 Rev 2:

RMF Step 1 — Prepare:
  Assign ISSO, System Owner, Authorizing Official. Establish privacy roles
  (SAOP, Privacy Officer). Define system boundary to include all components
  storing or processing HR data.

RMF Step 2 — Categorize:
  Document FIPS 199 categorization in the System Categorization document.
  Categorization = {(C, MODERATE), (I, MODERATE), (A, MODERATE)}.
  Overall: MODERATE.

RMF Step 3 — Select:
  Select SP 800-53B Moderate baseline. Apply PT family privacy overlay.
  Identify common controls inherited from the data center/agency infrastructure
  (PE, MA, CP controls may be partially or fully inheritable). Document in SSP.

RMF Step 4 — Implement:
  Implement Moderate baseline controls. Prioritize: AC (access control),
  IA (MFA), SC-8/SC-28 (encryption), AU (audit logging), PT (privacy),
  AC-6 (least privilege).

RMF Step 5 — Assess:
  Independent security assessment using SP 800-53A Rev 5 procedures.
  Include Privacy Impact Assessment review.

RMF Step 6 — Authorize:
  Authorizing Official issues ATO based on residual risk. Complete PIA and
  SORN publication prior to ATO.

RMF Step 7 — Monitor:
  Implement continuous monitoring strategy per CA-7. Conduct annual control
  assessments. Manage POA&M items for any control deficiencies found.
