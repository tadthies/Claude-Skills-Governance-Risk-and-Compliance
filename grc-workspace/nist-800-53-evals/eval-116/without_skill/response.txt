FIPS 199 CATEGORIZATION AND NIST SP 800-53 BASELINE SELECTION
Federal HR System — PII, Salary Data, and Performance Reviews (5,000 Staff)

==============================================================================
OVERVIEW
==============================================================================

This analysis walks through the FIPS 199 security categorization process for
a federal HR system storing employee PII, salary data, and performance reviews,
and then maps that categorization to the appropriate NIST SP 800-53 baseline.

==============================================================================
STEP 1: FIPS 199 SECURITY CATEGORIZATION
==============================================================================

FIPS 199 ("Standards for Security Categorization of Federal Information and
Information Systems") requires agencies to assess the potential impact of a
security breach across three security objectives:

  - Confidentiality: Preserving authorized restrictions on access and disclosure
  - Integrity: Guarding against improper modification or destruction
  - Availability: Ensuring timely and reliable access to information

For each objective, the potential impact is rated LOW, MODERATE, or HIGH.
The overall system categorization is determined by the "high-water mark" —
the highest impact level across all three objectives.

------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Information Types in Scope
------------------------------------------------------------------------------

This HR system contains the following information types (aligned to NIST SP
800-60, Volume II, "Guide for Mapping Types of Information and Information
Systems to Security Categories"):

  1. Personally Identifiable Information (PII) — employee records
     NIST 800-60 Type: C.3.1.1 — General Information (PII)

  2. Salary / Compensation Data — financial HR records
     NIST 800-60 Type: C.3.5.1 — Compensation Management (Pay and Benefits)

  3. Performance Reviews — employee appraisal records
     NIST 800-60 Type: C.3.1.2 — Human Resources Management (Performance)

------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Impact Analysis by Security Objective
------------------------------------------------------------------------------

CONFIDENTIALITY
  - Employee PII (names, SSNs, home addresses, dates of birth, etc.) is
    sensitive personal data protected under the Privacy Act of 1974. Unauthorized
    disclosure could harm 5,000 individuals through identity theft, financial
    harm, or reputational damage.
  - Salary data is sensitive and its exposure could cause significant
    embarrassment, workplace conflict, and individual financial harm.
  - Performance reviews contain evaluative judgments that could harm careers
    or violate privacy expectations if disclosed.
  - Impact Rating: MODERATE to HIGH. A breach affecting 5,000 federal employees
    involving SSNs and financial data would cause serious adverse effects.
    Most agencies categorize this type of system at MODERATE for confidentiality
    as the standard baseline, though agencies with a large workforce or highly
    sensitive positions may elect HIGH.
  - Recommended Rating: MODERATE (with consideration for HIGH if the workforce
    includes sensitive national security positions or the agency's risk
    tolerance requires it)

INTEGRITY
  - Corruption or unauthorized modification of salary data could result in
    improper payments, payroll fraud, or denial of correct compensation —
    causing direct financial harm to employees and the agency.
  - Modification of performance records could impair promotion decisions,
    disciplinary actions, or workforce management functions.
  - PII corruption could cause incorrect benefits enrollment or identity errors.
  - Impact Rating: MODERATE. Integrity failures would cause serious adverse
    effects on agency mission and individual employee welfare, but are unlikely
    to be catastrophic or irreversible in most scenarios.
  - Recommended Rating: MODERATE

AVAILABILITY
  - An HR system supporting 5,000 staff must be available for payroll
    processing cycles, benefits enrollment windows, and onboarding/offboarding
    workflows. Extended outages could disrupt pay, benefits, and HR operations.
  - However, the system does not typically support life-safety or real-time
    mission-critical operations (e.g., emergency response).
  - Impact Rating: MODERATE. Temporary unavailability would cause significant
    disruption but is unlikely to result in catastrophic harm.
  - Recommended Rating: MODERATE

------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Security Categorization Result
------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Applying the high-water mark principle per FIPS 199:

  SC HR_System = {(Confidentiality, MODERATE), (Integrity, MODERATE),
                  (Availability, MODERATE)}

  OVERALL SYSTEM CATEGORIZATION: MODERATE

This is expressed in FIPS 199 notation as:

  SC HR_System = {(confidentiality, MODERATE), (integrity, MODERATE),
                  (availability, MODERATE)} = MODERATE

NOTE: If the agency determines that the confidentiality impact should be HIGH
(e.g., due to inclusion of law enforcement personnel records, SCI-cleared staff
data, or whistleblower information), the overall categorization would become
HIGH, and a different baseline would apply. Agencies should document their
rationale in the System Security Plan (SSP).

==============================================================================
STEP 2: NIST SP 800-53 BASELINE SELECTION
==============================================================================

NIST SP 800-53 Rev. 5 ("Security and Privacy Controls for Information Systems
and Organizations") provides three control baselines tied directly to the FIPS
199 categorization:

  - LOW Baseline:      For systems categorized as LOW overall
  - MODERATE Baseline: For systems categorized as MODERATE overall
  - HIGH Baseline:     For systems categorized as HIGH overall

Based on the MODERATE overall categorization above:

  SELECTED BASELINE: MODERATE

The NIST SP 800-53 Rev. 5 MODERATE baseline includes approximately 323 control
requirements (base controls plus required enhancements) across 20 control
families. Key control families particularly relevant to this HR system include:

  AC  — Access Control
        Enforce least-privilege access; role-based access control for HR
        personnel, managers, and system administrators. Restrict access to PII
        to only authorized users.

  AT  — Awareness and Training
        Security awareness training for all users handling PII and salary data.

  AU  — Audit and Accountability
        Log and review access to PII, salary records, and performance data.
        Audit logs must be protected against tampering.

  CA  — Assessment, Authorization, and Monitoring
        Conduct security assessments; obtain Authority to Operate (ATO) from
        the Authorizing Official (AO); continuous monitoring program.

  CM  — Configuration Management
        Maintain baselines for the HR application, database, and server
        configurations.

  CP  — Contingency Planning
        Backup and recovery procedures for PII and HR data; test recovery
        capabilities; define Recovery Time Objective (RTO) and Recovery Point
        Objective (RPO).

  IA  — Identification and Authentication
        Multi-factor authentication (MFA) for all privileged users and remote
        access; strong password policies; PIV/CAC authentication where required
        by HSPD-12.

  IR  — Incident Response
        Documented incident response plan that specifically addresses PII
        breaches, including OMB Memorandum M-17-12 breach notification
        requirements (report to US-CERT within 1 hour of discovery).

  MP  — Media Protection
        Protect and sanitize removable media; encrypt data at rest on storage
        media containing PII.

  PE  — Physical and Environmental Protection
        Physical access controls to the internal data center; visitor controls;
        environmental safeguards (fire, flood, HVAC).

  PL  — Planning
        System Security Plan (SSP) documenting the security architecture,
        control implementation, and interconnections.

  PS  — Personnel Security
        Screening of individuals with access to PII; rules of behavior; access
        termination procedures for separated employees.

  PT  — PII Processing and Transparency (Privacy Controls — Rev. 5 addition)
        Privacy controls specifically addressing lawful collection, use, and
        retention of PII; Privacy Act System of Records Notice (SORN); Privacy
        Impact Assessment (PIA) required per E-Government Act of 2002.

  RA  — Risk Assessment
        System-level risk assessment; vulnerability scanning; supply chain
        risk management.

  SA  — System and Services Acquisition
        Secure development practices; security requirements in acquisition
        contracts; software assurance.

  SC  — System and Communications Protection
        Encryption of PII and salary data in transit (TLS 1.2+); network
        segmentation; boundary protection.

  SI  — System and Information Integrity
        Malware protection; security alerts; flaw remediation; spam protection.

STEP 3: ADDITIONAL COMPLIANCE OBLIGATIONS
==============================================================================

Beyond the NIST SP 800-53 MODERATE baseline, this system triggers several
additional federal requirements:

  1. PRIVACY ACT OF 1974
     The system constitutes a "System of Records" under the Privacy Act because
     it retrieves employee information by personal identifier. The agency must:
     - Publish a System of Records Notice (SORN) in the Federal Register
     - Establish a routine uses schedule
     - Respond to Privacy Act requests

  2. PRIVACY IMPACT ASSESSMENT (PIA)
     Required under Section 208 of the E-Government Act of 2002. Must be
     completed before deployment and updated when the system changes
     significantly.

  3. OMB CIRCULAR A-130 (Managing Information as a Strategic Resource)
     Requires senior agency official for privacy (SAOP) involvement, privacy
     continuous monitoring, and privacy risk management.

  4. FISMA (Federal Information Security Modernization Act of 2014)
     Requires an Authority to Operate (ATO) from the Authorizing Official.
     The ATO package must include: SSP, Security Assessment Report (SAR),
     Plan of Action and Milestones (POA&M).

  5. OMB M-17-12 (Preparing for and Responding to a Breach of PII)
     Establishes breach response requirements including US-CERT notification
     within 1 hour and affected-individual notification timelines.

  6. HSPD-12 / PIV
     Federal employees must authenticate using Personal Identity Verification
     (PIV) cards for logical access to federal systems where technically
     feasible.

  7. NIST SP 800-122 (Guide to Protecting the Confidentiality of PII)
     Provides supplemental guidance specific to PII protection, including
     de-identification and minimization techniques.

==============================================================================
STEP 4: RECOMMENDED IMPLEMENTATION APPROACH
==============================================================================

  1. Conduct a formal FIPS 199 categorization workshop with the system owner,
     information owner, and ISSO — document all information types and impact
     ratings in the SSP.

  2. Register the system in the agency's GRC tool (e.g., CSAM, Xacta, or
     similar) under the MODERATE baseline.

  3. Tailor the MODERATE baseline as needed:
     - Apply organization-defined parameters (ODPs) per agency policy
     - Add compensating controls where required
     - Scoping guidance: exclude controls not applicable to the internal
       data center hosting model (e.g., some cloud-specific controls)

  4. Complete the Privacy Impact Assessment and submit the SORN for
     Federal Register publication prior to system go-live.

  5. Conduct security assessment (typically by an independent assessor or
     the agency's A&A team) against the tailored MODERATE baseline.

  6. Obtain ATO from the Authorizing Official before processing live PII.

  7. Establish a continuous monitoring strategy per NIST SP 800-137 aligned
     to the MODERATE categorization.

==============================================================================
SUMMARY
==============================================================================

  FIPS 199 Categorization:  MODERATE
                             {(Confidentiality, MODERATE),
                              (Integrity, MODERATE),
                              (Availability, MODERATE)}

  NIST SP 800-53 Baseline:  MODERATE (~323 controls and enhancements)

  Key Additional Frameworks: Privacy Act SORN, PIA (E-Gov Act), FISMA ATO,
                              OMB M-17-12, HSPD-12/PIV, NIST SP 800-122

  Hosting Consideration:    Internal data center hosting is fully compatible
                             with the MODERATE baseline. The agency retains
                             full control over physical and environmental
                             controls (PE family) and does not need to apply
                             FedRAMP authorization processes applicable to
                             cloud service providers.
