# NZISM Certification & Accreditation Gap Analysis — RESTRICTED System

The NZISM is issued by the **GCSB (National Cyber Security Centre — NCSC NZ)** as the New Zealand Government's mandatory information security manual. Its controls carry **MUST/SHOULD compliance requirements tied to the system's classification level** — for a RESTRICTED system, essential (MUST) controls cannot be waived without formal risk acceptance recorded by the Accreditation Authority. Certification and Accreditation (C&A) is mandatory for any system handling Restricted and above before it goes live.

## What the NZISM C&A Process Requires

C&A has two distinct stages that must not be conflated:

- **Certification** — the technical assessment confirming NZISM controls are implemented *and effective*. This requires independent validation/testing evidence, not just documentation.
- **Accreditation** — the formal acceptance of residual risk by the Accreditation Authority (typically the agency head or a delegate), granting Authorisation to Operate (ATO).

The full pathway:

1. **System Security Plan (SSP/SecPlan)** — documents the system boundary, classification, security objectives, and all implemented controls.
2. **Security Risk Management Plan (SRMP)** — identifies threats, vulnerabilities, likelihood, impact, treatments, and residual risk. This is a **mandatory** C&A artefact alongside the SSP, not optional.
3. **Control validation** — independent technical review verifying controls are implemented and effective (testing evidence required).
4. **Certification review and sign-off** — the ITSM (IT Security Manager) and CISO review validation evidence and formally certify the system.
5. **Plan of Action & Milestones (POA&M)** — documents and tracks remediation of any assessment findings.
6. **Accreditation decision** — the Accreditation Authority reviews residual risk and grants ATO, recorded formally.
7. **Ongoing monitoring** — continuous control monitoring and periodic re-certification (typically every 1–3 years depending on risk, or triggered earlier by major change, incident, or classification change).

## Summary of Critical Gaps

Your agency currently cannot proceed to certification, let alone accreditation, for three interlocking reasons:

1. **No SRMP.** This is a mandatory artefact, not a nice-to-have. Without it, residual risk cannot be identified or presented to the Accreditation Authority.
2. **No control validation testing.** Partial implementation without testing evidence means the ITSM/CISO have nothing independently verifiable to certify against.
3. **No ITSM sign-off**, which is a direct consequence of gaps 1 and 2 — certification review cannot occur until the SRMP exists and validation testing has been completed.

## Recommended Remediation Priority

1. **Immediately**: Commission the SRMP — threat/vulnerability identification, likelihood/impact assessment, proposed treatments, and residual risk statement, scoped to the RESTRICTED classification.
2. **In parallel**: Complete implementation of outstanding Restricted-tier controls (encryption at rest, MFA for remote access, privileged access separation, enhanced logging with 12-month retention, physical secure zone controls, supplier contractual NZISM-equivalent clauses).
3. **Next**: Commission independent control validation/testing against the SSP and NZISM control set — this produces the evidence the ITSM/CISO need.
4. **Then**: ITSM and CISO conduct certification review, sign off, and raise a POA&M for any residual findings.
5. **Finally**: Submit the certification package (SSP, SRMP, validation evidence, POA&M) to the Accreditation Authority for the formal accreditation decision and ATO.
