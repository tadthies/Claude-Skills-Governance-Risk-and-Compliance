# AI Risk Register — Credit Scoring Model (Financial Services)

Below is a working AI risk register built on the NIST AI RMF structure — columns are aligned to MAP (identification), MEASURE (TEVV), and MANAGE (treatment) so every entry traces cleanly back to the framework. Treatments are connected to model risk management practice under **SR 11-7 / OCC 2011-12**: independent validation before use, champion–challenger comparison in production, ongoing monitoring, and documented effective challenge at the model risk committee. ## Treatment Options Reference

- **Mitigate** — technical (retrain, remove proxy features, add human review), operational (restrict use case, tighten thresholds), or process controls that reduce likelihood/impact
- **Transfer** — contractual risk-shifting to vendor (indemnification, warranty on fair-lending testing, audit rights) — relevant for R-03
- **Avoid** — decommission or decline to deploy a feature/model version where residual risk cannot be brought within tolerance
- **Accept** — formally document and obtain Model Risk Committee / CRO sign-off for any residual risk below full mitigation, per MANAGE 1.3

## Financial-Services Model Risk Alignment (SR 11-7 / OCC 2011-12)

Every treatment above should map to standard MRM lifecycle controls:
- **Independent validation** before initial production use and after any material model change (applies to R-01, R-02, R-05)
- **Champion–challenger comparison** in production, particularly for the vendor-hosted model (R-03) and ongoing drift monitoring (R-04)
- **Ongoing monitoring** with defined thresholds and escalation triggers (R-04, R-03)
- **Effective challenge** documented at the Model Risk Committee — the committee (not the model owner alone) should be the accepting authority for any residual risk above tolerance (MANAGE 1.3), consistent with SR 11-7's requirement for independent, skeptical review

Update the Likelihood/Impact and Treatment columns whenever MEASURE produces new evidence (MEASURE 4.3), and log outcomes back into MANAGE 4 for the quarterly model risk review.
