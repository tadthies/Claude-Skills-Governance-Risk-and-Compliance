# AI Risk Register Template — Credit Scoring Model (Financial Services)

This is a working risk register template designed for a financial institution deploying a machine learning credit scoring model. It maps each risk to the **NIST AI Risk Management Framework (AI RMF 1.0)** functions and categories (GOVERN, MAP, MEASURE, MANAGE) and cross-references relevant financial services regulatory drivers (ECOA/Reg B, FCRA, SR 11-7/OCC 2011-12, GLBA) where applicable. It is intended as a starting point to be tailored, populated with real ownership/dates, and integrated into your enterprise risk management (ERM) and model risk management (MRM) systems.

---

## How to Use This Register

- **Risk ID**: Unique identifier (prefix by category, e.g., FL = Fair Lending, MD = Model/Data, TP = Third Party, CY = Cyber, PR = Privacy, RC = Regulatory Compliance, OP = Operational).
- **AI RMF Mapping**: The primary NIST AI RMF 1.0 subcategory(ies) the risk and its controls relate to. Most risks touch multiple functions (GOVERN is nearly always relevant as the umbrella); the mapping below lists the *primary* driving subcategory plus key secondary ones.
- **Likelihood / Impact**: Score 1 (Low) – 5 (Very High). Adjust scale to match your enterprise risk taxonomy.
- **Risk Rating**: Likelihood x Impact, banded as Low (1–6), Medium (7–12), High (13–19), Critical (20–25).
- **Treatment Option**: Mitigate / Transfer / Avoid / Accept.
- **Treatment Actions/Owner**: Concrete control activities and the accountable role (not a named individual — map to your governance structure).

Recommended review cadence: quarterly for High/Critical risks, semi-annually for Medium, annually for Low, plus ad hoc review upon model change, material data drift, or regulatory guidance updates.

## NIST AI RMF Function Reference (for mapping consistency)

- **GOVERN** — Culture, policies, accountability, roles, risk tolerance, third-party risk, and documentation practices that cut across the AI lifecycle. Nearly every risk in this register has a GOVERN component as the accountability backbone.
- **MAP** — Establishing context: intended use, impacted individuals/groups, legal/regulatory requirements, and appropriateness of data/model for the use case.
- **MEASURE** — Testing, evaluation, and monitoring: bias/fairness metrics, performance, robustness, security, explainability, and ongoing monitoring.
- **MANAGE** — Risk response: prioritization, resource allocation, incident response, third-party risk treatment, and continuous improvement based on MEASURE outputs.

---

## Notes on Treatment Options

- **Mitigate**: Reduce likelihood or impact through controls (most common for credit scoring risks given regulatory obligations that can't be waived).
- **Transfer**: Shift risk via insurance, vendor contractual indemnification, or outsourcing (limited applicability for fair lending risk, which cannot be fully transferred away from the institution).
- **Avoid**: Decline to deploy a feature, data source, or model architecture that creates unacceptable risk (e.g., NU-01).
- **Accept**: Formally accept residual risk within risk appetite after mitigation, with sign-off from appropriate risk committee (not illustrated above as a primary treatment given the regulated nature of credit decisioning, but applicable to low-rated residual risks after controls are applied).
