## 3. Estimated SPRS Score Impact

SPRS (Supplier Performance Risk System) scoring starts at **110** (perfect) and subtracts points per unimplemented practice (weighted 1, 3, or 5 points depending on practice criticality per DoD Assessment Methodology).

Estimated deductions based on stated gaps:

| Gap | Likely Practice(s) | Typical Weight | Est. Points Lost |
|---|---|---|---|
| MFA not enforced org-wide (only email) | 3.5.3, 3.1.12/3.1.13/3.1.18 | 5-point items | -10 to -15 |
| No FIPS-validated encryption for CUI at rest | 3.13.11, 3.13.16 | 5-point items | -10 |
| Flat network / CUI on shared drives (no segmentation, likely weak access enforcement) | 3.1.3, 3.13.1, 3.1.1/3.1.2 exposure | 1–5 point items | -5 to -8 |
| IR plan untested | 3.6.3 | 1-point item | -1 |
| SSP outdated (may cascade — if SSP doesn't reflect environment, many practices may be mis-scored or unverifiable) | 3.12.4 | 1-point item (but assessment risk is systemic) | -1 (direct), high indirect risk |

**Rough estimated current SPRS score: 65–80 out of 110**, depending on how strictly the flat-network/shared-drive CUI exposure is scored across multiple AC/SC practices, and assuming the "Unknown" domains (PE, AU, MA) are reasonably implemented. This is a working estimate only — an actual score requires practice-by-practice scoring against the DoD Assessment Methodology.

A score in this range is a **material contract risk**: many DoD solicitations now require a current, satisfactory SPRS score (often with a defined minimum, e.g., 88 or better, or an approved POA&M) prior to award. A score in the 65–80 range without a credible POA&M closure timeline would likely be disqualifying or trigger scrutiny.

## 4. Prioritized Remediation Plan

### Tier 1 — Critical / 0–30 days (highest SPRS point recovery, highest risk reduction)

1. **Update the System Security Plan (SSP)**
   - Refresh to reflect actual current-state environment, boundary, and CUI flow.
   - This is a prerequisite for accurate self-assessment and for building a valid POA&M.
   - Practice: CA.L2-3.12.4. *Quick win, unlocks everything else.*

2. **Stand up a Plan of Action & Milestones (POA&M)**
   - Formally document every gap below with owner, milestone dates, and interim risk mitigations.
   - Required to support SPRS submission and demonstrate a credible path to Level 2.

3. **Extend MFA beyond cloud email**
   - Enforce MFA for all privileged accounts, remote access (VPN/RDP), and any system processing/storing CUI.
   - Practice: IA.L2-3.5.3, AC.L2-3.1.12/3.1.13. *High point value, typically achievable quickly with existing IdP (Entra ID/Okta/Duo).*

### Tier 2 — High Priority / 30–90 days

4. **Implement FIPS 140-2/140-3 validated encryption for CUI at rest**
   - Identify all repositories (shared drives, servers, endpoints) storing CUI.
   - Deploy validated encryption (e.g., BitLocker with FIPS mode, validated full-disk/volume encryption, or a CUI enclave solution).
   - Practice: SC.L2-3.13.11, 3.13.16. *High point value — critical path item.*

5. **Segment the network and relocate/restrict CUI**
   - Move CUI off general shared drives into a dedicated, access-controlled enclave (or a GCC High/CMMC-ready cloud environment).
   - Implement VLANs/subnetting to separate CUI systems from general IT.
   - Apply least-privilege access controls (AC.L2-3.1.1–3.1.5) to the CUI enclave.
   - Practice: SC.L2-3.13.1/3.13.5, AC.L2-3.1.3. *Largest architectural lift — consider a scoped CUI enclave to reduce overall assessment boundary and cost.*

6. **Test the Incident Response Plan**
   - Conduct a tabletop exercise within 90 days; document results, lessons learned, and update the plan.
   - Practice: IR.L2-3.6.3. *Low cost, meaningful point recovery, low effort relative to impact.*

### Tier 3 — Medium Priority / 90–180 days

7. **Formal Risk Assessment refresh**
   - Conduct/update a risk assessment aligned to the revised SSP and network changes (RA.L2-3.11.1–3.11.3).

8. **Media protection controls**
   - Define and implement media marking, sanitization, and CUI storage handling procedures for the relocated CUI enclave (MP.L2-3.8.x).

9. **Configuration management baseline**
   - Document baseline configs for CUI-boundary systems post-segmentation; implement change control (CM.L2-3.4.x).

10. **Audit logging and review** (if not already covered — flagged as "Unknown" above)
    - Confirm AU.L2 practices (log generation, review, retention) are in place for the CUI enclave.

### Tier 4 — Maintain / Ongoing

11. **Continue annual security awareness training**, but consider adding role-based training for privileged/IT users and CUI handlers (AT.L2-3.2.2).
12. **Continue background screening program** — already compliant; maintain and document consistently (PS.L2-3.9.1/3.9.2).
13. **Recurring SSP review cadence** — annual minimum, or upon any significant environment change, to prevent recurrence of staleness.

## 6. Key Recommendations

- **Do not delay the SSP/POA&M refresh** — it is both a quick win and the foundation for every other action; DoD contracts increasingly require a current SSP and an active POA&M with realistic milestones as a condition of award.
- **Prioritize the CUI enclave/segmentation strategy** — isolating CUI onto a smaller, tightly controlled environment (rather than remediating the entire flat network) can significantly reduce both cost and assessment scope.
- **Treat MFA and encryption as parallel, not sequential, workstreams** — both are high-point, high-risk items achievable within 30–90 days with typical enterprise tooling.
- **Engage a C3PAO or CMMC Registered Practitioner (RP) early**, particularly before finalizing the POA&M, to validate scoring assumptions and avoid rework ahead of a formal Level 2 assessment.
- **Budget for a FIPS-validated encryption solution and network segmentation** as the two largest cost/time drivers in this remediation plan.
