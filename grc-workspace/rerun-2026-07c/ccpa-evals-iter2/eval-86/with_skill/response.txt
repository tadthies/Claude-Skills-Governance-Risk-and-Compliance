# CCPA/CPRA Applicability Analysis

## Are you subject to CCPA/CPRA?

**Yes.** A for-profit business is covered if it meets **at least one** of three thresholds:

| # | Threshold | Exact Figure | Your Facts |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Annual gross revenue | Exceeds **$25 million** in the preceding calendar year | You're at **$30M** — exceeds threshold |
| 2 | Data volume | Annually buys, sells, receives, or shares PI of **100,000+** consumers/households | Your **80,000 CA customers/year** does *not* independently meet this threshold |
| 3 | Revenue from data monetization | Derives **50%+** of annual revenue from selling/sharing PI | Not indicated — assumed not met |

Because you only need to meet **one** threshold, your **$30M in annual gross revenue** alone brings you within scope of CCPA/CPRA, regardless of the fact that your California customer data volume (80,000) falls short of the 100,000-consumer/household threshold. You are a covered "business" under Cal. Civ. Code §1798.100 et seq. (CCPA, effective 2020) as amended by the CPRA (effective 2023).

*Note: revenue and data-volume thresholds must be reassessed annually* — if your California customer base grows past 100,000/year that's a second independent basis for coverage, but it isn't necessary here since you already qualify on revenue.

## Core Obligations

### 1. Classify your downstream data recipients
Before anything else, determine who you share PI with and how:

| Classification | Definition | Sale? |
|---|---|---|
| **Service Provider** | Processes PI on your behalf under a contract prohibiting further use beyond the specified business purpose | Not a sale |
| **Contractor** (CPRA) | Receives PI under a contract prohibiting other use; must certify compliance | Not a sale |
| **Third Party** | Receives PI but isn't a service provider or contractor | May constitute a sale or sharing |

Vendor classifications should be reassessed whenever a contract is renewed or a new recipient is onboarded.

### 2. Build consumer rights fulfillment processes
You must be able to honor:
- **Right to Know** — specific pieces/categories of PI, sources, purposes, third parties (45 days + optional 45-day extension)
- **Right to Delete** — delete PI, propagate to service providers/contractors, subject to enumerated exceptions (45 days + extension)
- **Right to Correct** (CPRA) — correct inaccurate PI (45 days + extension)
- **Right to Opt-Out of Sale/Sharing** — "Do Not Sell or Share" link, honor GPC signals, effectuate within 15 business days
- **Right to Limit SPI Use** (CPRA) — if you use sensitive personal information beyond permitted purposes (15 business days)
- **Right to Non-Discrimination** — cannot penalize consumers for exercising rights

**Response timeline (applies across all request types):** confirm receipt within **10 business days**, describing your verification process; substantive response within **45 calendar days**, extendable once by another 45 days with notice; opt-out/limit requests effectuated within **15 business days**.

**Intake channels:** provide at least two request-submission methods (an e-commerce business can typically use a web form plus email; a toll-free number is required where applicable).

**Identity verification:** 2-data-point match for standard requests (know categories, delete non-sensitive data); 3-data-point match plus signed perjury declaration for sensitive requests (specific pieces of PI, deletion of sensitive data). Opt-out requests require no identity verification beyond reasonable confirmation.

**Free of charge:** twice per 12-month period.

### 3. Publish required notices
- **Privacy Notice at Collection** — categories of PI collected, purposes, whether sold/shared, retention periods, link to full policy — delivered at or before collection
- **Privacy Policy** — comprehensive disclosures on categories, purposes, third parties, consumer rights, contact info, and the "Do Not Sell or Share My Personal Information" link; update annually

### 4. Stand up opt-out mechanisms
- "Do Not Sell or Share My Personal Information" homepage link
- Honor **Global Privacy Control (GPC)** signals as a valid opt-out
- "Limit the Use of My Sensitive Personal Information" link, if applicable
- **ADMT opt-out** — only relevant if you use automated decision-making with legal/significant effects; regulations effective January 1, 2026, implementation deadline **January 1, 2027**

### 5. Apply data minimization, purpose limitation, and retention limits (CPRA)
Only collect PI adequate, relevant, and limited to your disclosed purpose; disclose and adhere to retention periods; don't repurpose PI for undisclosed uses.

### 6. Update vendor contracts
Contracts with service providers/contractors must include purpose limitations, prohibition on further sale/sharing, cooperation with consumer requests, audit rights, and deletion obligations.

### 7. Cybersecurity audits & risk assessments (effective January 1, 2026)
If your processing presents **significant risk** to consumers, you must conduct **annual cybersecurity audits** and document **risk assessments** before high-risk processing, producible to the CPPA on request. As an e-commerce business handling payment and customer data at meaningful scale, you should evaluate whether this applies to you now that the regulations are live.

## Penalty Exposure

- Unintentional violations: up to **$2,500 per violation**
- Intentional violations: up to **$7,500 per violation**
- Violations involving minors' PI: up to **$7,500 per violation** (always treated as intentional)
- **Private right of action** (data breach only): $100–$750 per consumer per incident, class-action eligible
- A **30-day cure period** applies to AG actions

Recent CPPA enforcement (2026) illustrates real exposure at your scale: **Ford Motor Company was fined $375K** specifically for failing to process deletion and access requests within the required timeframes — a reminder that missing the 45-day deadline is itself an actionable violation, not just a procedural lapse. Given you're an e-commerce business relying on ad tech/analytics vendors, also review whether any of those integrations constitute a "sale" or "sharing," since **The Walt Disney Company's $2.75M fine** (the largest CCPA enforcement to date) involved third-party ad tech data-sharing deficiencies.

## Recommended Next Steps
1. Document your threshold determination (revenue basis) and file it for annual reassessment.
2. Inventory all PI flows and classify every downstream recipient (service provider / contractor / third party).
3. Stand up or audit your rights-intake process against the 10-business-day acknowledgment / 45-day response deadlines.
4. Confirm your homepage has a working "Do Not Sell or Share" link and GPC signal handling.
5. Evaluate whether your processing triggers the cybersecurity audit and risk assessment obligations effective January 1, 2026.
6. Refresh your privacy notice at collection and privacy policy to ensure CPRA-era disclosures (retention periods, SPI categories, etc.) are current.
